## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Unscramble the following words to form a grammatically correct Spanish sentence. Then, identify the Subject, Verb, and Object.

Words: `bebe`, `leche`, `el gato`
(Verb `bebe` means "drinks")

**Exercise 2**
Translate the following simple English sentence into Spanish, ensuring you follow the Subject-Verb-Object pattern.

Sentence: "I have a question."
(The verb "to have" is `tener`, and the "I" form is `tengo`. "Question" is `pregunta`, which is a feminine noun.)

**Exercise 3**
A fellow student has written two sentences, but only one uses the standard Subject-Verb-Object word order correctly. Identify the correct sentence and explain why the other is incorrect based on the S-V-O pattern.

A) `La película mira Juan.`
B) `Juan mira la película.`
(The verb `mira` means "watches".)

**Exercise 4**
You want to say "We see the big houses." In Spanish, the verb "we see" is `vemos`. The noun "houses" is `casas` (feminine), and the adjective for "big" is `grande`.

Construct the correct Spanish sentence. Explain your choice of word order for the object and its adjective.

**Exercise 5**
Imagine you are at a cafe. You see a man writing some important letters. Combine your knowledge of articles, noun gender, and adjective agreement to write a single, complete Spanish sentence describing this scene.

Use the following elements:
*   Subject: `el hombre` (the man)
*   Verb: `escribe` (writes)
*   Object Noun: `cartas` (letters, feminine)
*   Adjective: `importante` (important)
*   Article for the object: Indefinite (some letters)

**Exercise 6**
A friend is asking you what Maria is doing. You see that she is reading a book, and you know the book is old. How would you reply in one complete Spanish sentence that follows the Subject-Verb-Object structure? Your sentence must correctly use the verb `leer` (to read), choose the appropriate article for "book" (`libro`, masculine), and ensure the adjective "old" (`viejo`) agrees with the noun it describes.

(The form of `leer` for "she reads" is `lee`.)

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
**Correct Sentence:** `El gato bebe leche.`

**Reasoning:**
*   **Subject:** `El gato` (The cat) - This is the noun performing the action.
*   **Verb:** `bebe` (drinks) - This is the action.
*   **Object:** `leche` (milk) - This is the noun receiving the action.
The sentence follows the standard Subject-Verb-Object order.

**Answer 2**
**Correct Sentence:** `Yo tengo una pregunta.`

**Reasoning:**
*   **Subject:** `Yo` (I)
*   **Verb:** `tengo` (have)
*   **Object:** `una pregunta` (a question)
The sentence correctly follows the S-V-O structure. It also uses the correct indefinite article `una` because `pregunta` is a singular, feminine noun.

**Answer 3**
**Correct Sentence:** B) `Juan mira la película.`

**Reasoning:**
*   Sentence B follows the correct Subject-Verb-Object pattern: `Juan` (Subject) `mira` (Verb) `la película` (Object).
*   Sentence A, `La película mira Juan`, follows an Object-Verb-Subject (O-V-S) pattern. While this word order can be used in Spanish for emphasis or in poetic contexts, it is not the standard, fundamental structure for a simple affirmative sentence.

**Answer 4**
**Correct Sentence:** `Nosotros vemos las casas grandes.`

**Reasoning:**
*   The sentence is assembled in the Subject-Verb-Object order: `Nosotros` (S) `vemos` (V) `las casas grandes` (O).
*   The object itself (`las casas grandes`) is constructed correctly. `Casas` is a plural, feminine noun, so it requires the plural feminine article `las`.
*   In Spanish, adjectives typically follow the noun they describe. The adjective `grande` becomes `grandes` to agree in number (plural) with `casas`.

**Answer 5**
**Correct Sentence:** `El hombre escribe unas cartas importantes.`

**Reasoning:**
*   The sentence follows the S-V-O structure: `El hombre` (Subject) `escribe` (Verb) `unas cartas importantes` (Object).
*   The object is formed by integrating prior concepts:
    *   **Article:** `cartas` is plural and feminine, so the indefinite article is `unas` ("some").
    *   **Adjective Agreement:** The adjective `importante` must be made plural (`importantes`) to agree with the plural noun `cartas`. It follows the noun, which is the standard position for descriptive adjectives.

**Answer 6**
**Correct Sentence:** `Maria lee un libro viejo.`

**Reasoning:**
*   This sentence correctly synthesizes all the required concepts into the S-V-O structure.
*   **Subject:** `Maria`
*   **Verb:** `lee` (the correct conjugation for "she reads")
*   **Object:** `un libro viejo` (an old book). This part shows correct application of several rules:
    *   An indefinite article `un` is used because it's "a book," not a specific, previously mentioned book. `un` is chosen because `libro` is masculine and singular.
    *   The adjective `viejo` comes after the noun `libro`.
    *   `viejo` is in its masculine, singular form to agree with `libro`.
