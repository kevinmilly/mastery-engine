## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Make the following singular Spanish nouns plural: `libro`, `mesa`, `profesor`, `clase`.

**Exercise 2**
For each noun below, determine if it is masculine or feminine and explain your reasoning based on its ending: `chico`, `ventana`, `problema`, `mano`.

**Exercise 3**
A friend is practicing Spanish and describes a city with many universities by saying `muchas universidads`. Is the plural form `universidads` correct? Explain why or why not, and provide the correct plural form.

**Exercise 4**
Group the following nouns into "Masculine" and "Feminine" columns. For at least one word in each column, explain the rule or pattern that helped you decide its gender.

Nouns: `nacionalidad`, `idioma`, `foto`, `lápiz`, `flor`.

**Exercise 5**
You have learned the subject pronouns (`él`, `ella`, `ellos`, `ellas`). Match the correct pronoun to refer to each of the following groups of people. Explain your choice based on the gender and number of the nouns in each group.

1.  el profesor y la estudiante
2.  la mujer y la doctora
3.  el conductor

**Exercise 6**
You are describing a family photo. In the photo, there is one man (`hombre`), one woman (`mujer`), and two children (one boy and one girl). If you had to use a single Spanish noun to describe the two children together, what word would you use? Now, if you had to use a single pronoun (`ellos` or `ellas`) to refer to the entire group (the man, the woman, and the two children), which would you choose? Justify your answers based on the rules of grammatical gender in Spanish.

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The correct plural forms are: `libros`, `mesas`, `profesores`, `clases`.

*   **Reasoning:** Nouns that end in a vowel (`libro`, `mesa`, `clase`) are made plural by adding **-s**. Nouns that end in a consonant (`profesor`) are made plural by adding **-es**.

**Answer 2**
*   `chico` is **masculine**. **Reasoning:** It ends in -o, which is the standard ending for masculine nouns.
*   `ventana` is **feminine**. **Reasoning:** It ends in -a, which is the standard ending for feminine nouns.
*   `problema` is **masculine**. **Reasoning:** This is a common exception. Many nouns that end in -ma (of Greek origin) are masculine, despite the -a ending.
*   `mano` is **feminine**. **Reasoning:** This is another common exception. Despite its -o ending, `mano` is a feminine noun.

**Answer 3**
The plural form `universidads` is **incorrect**.

*   **Reasoning:** The singular noun is `universidad`, which ends in a consonant (`d`). To make nouns ending in a consonant plural, you must add **-es**, not just -s.
*   **Correct form:** `universidades`.

**Answer 4**
**Masculine:**
*   `idioma`
*   `lápiz`

**Feminine:**
*   `nacionalidad`
*   `foto`
*   `flor`

*   **Reasoning Samples:**
    *   **Masculine:** `idioma` is masculine because many nouns ending in `-ma` are exceptions to the `-a` is feminine rule. `lápiz` is masculine (by convention) and ends in a consonant.
    *   **Feminine:** `nacionalidad` is feminine because nouns ending in `-dad` are typically feminine. `foto` is a feminine exception to the `-o` rule because it is a shortened form of `la fotografía`. `flor` ends in a consonant and is feminine by convention.

**Answer 5**
1.  **`ellos`**: `El profesor` (one male) and `la estudiante` (one female) form a mixed-gender group. In Spanish, the masculine plural pronoun `ellos` is used to refer to a mixed-gender group.
2.  **`ellas`**: `La mujer` (one female) and `la doctora` (one female) form an all-female group. The feminine plural pronoun `ellas` is used to refer to a group of all females.
3.  **`él`**: `El conductor` (the driver) is a singular, masculine noun. The correct singular, masculine pronoun is `él`.

**Answer 6**
*   **For the two children:** You would use the noun `niños`.
    *   **Reasoning:** Even though the group consists of one boy (`niño`) and one girl (`niña`), the masculine plural form is used to refer to a mixed-gender group.

*   **For the entire group:** You would use the pronoun `ellos`.
    *   **Reasoning:** The entire group consists of a man, a woman, a boy, and a girl (`hombre`, `mujer`, `niño`, `niña`). Since this is a mixed-gender group of more than one person, the masculine plural pronoun `ellos` must be used to refer to them all together.
