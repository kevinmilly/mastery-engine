## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Your friend is learning Spanish and writes the sentence: "La flor es bonito." The adjective they are trying to use is `bonito` (pretty). Is their sentence correct? If not, correct it and explain why your correction is necessary.

**Exercise 2**
Complete the phrase below with the correct form of the adjective `pequeño` (small). Explain how you determined the correct ending.

`Los gatos son ___________.`

**Exercise 3**
You want to describe a student (`un estudiante`) and a class (`una clase`) using the adjective `grande` (big). Write the two correct phrases (e.g., "the big student," "the big class"). Explain why the form of `grande` is the same or different in each phrase.

**Exercise 4**
Imagine you are describing a group consisting of three women (`tres mujeres`) and one man (`un hombre`). If you want to describe the entire group as "kind" using the adjective `amable`, which form should you use: `amable` or `amables`? Now, if you use the adjective `alto` (tall), which form should you use: `altos` or `altas`? Explain your reasoning for both choices.

**Exercise 5**
Unscramble the following words to form a complete, grammatically correct sentence. Explain the two key grammatical changes you had to make to the base forms of the words provided.

Words: `ser / una / chicas / inteligente / las / .`

**Exercise 6**
You see two new, modern buildings. Using your knowledge of the verb `ser`, articles, and adjective agreement, write one complete Spanish sentence to describe them. Your sentence must use the noun `edificios` (buildings) and both of the adjectives `moderno` (modern) and `nuevo` (new). Justify the form you chose for each adjective.

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The sentence is incorrect. The correct sentence is: "La flor es **bonita**."

**Reasoning:**
The noun `flor` (flower) is feminine, which we know from the feminine definite article `la`. Adjectives must agree in gender with the nouns they describe. The adjective `bonito` ends in -o, indicating it is the masculine form. To make it agree with the feminine noun `flor`, we must change the ending from -o to -a, resulting in `bonita`.

**Answer 2**
The completed phrase is: "Los gatos son **pequeños**."

**Reasoning:**
1.  **Gender:** The noun `gatos` (cats) is masculine, indicated by the article `los`. Therefore, the adjective must also be masculine, ending in -o.
2.  **Number:** The noun `gatos` is plural, also indicated by the -s ending on the article (`los`) and the noun itself. To make the adjective agree in number, we must add an -s to the masculine form `pequeño`, resulting in `pequeños`.

**Answer 3**
The two phrases are:
1.  `el estudiante grande` (the big student)
2.  `la clase grande` (the big class)

**Reasoning:**
The adjective `grande` is the same in both phrases because adjectives that end in -e (or most other consonants) do not change their ending for gender. `Grande` can be used to describe both masculine nouns (like `estudiante`) and feminine nouns (like `clase`) without modification. It would only change for number (e.g., `los estudiantes grandes`).

**Answer 4**
For `amable` (kind), you would use **`amables`**.
For `alto` (tall), you would use **`altos`**.

**Reasoning:**
1.  **`amables`**: The adjective `amable` ends in -e, so it does not change for gender. Since the group has more than one person, we only need to make it plural by adding -s.
2.  **`altos`**: The adjective `alto` ends in -o, so it must agree in both gender and number. In Spanish, when a group is of mixed gender (even one man and many women), the masculine plural form of the adjective is used. Therefore, we use the masculine (`alto`) and make it plural (`altos`).

**Answer 5**
The correct sentence is: "**Las chicas son inteligentes.**"

**Reasoning:**
1.  **Verb Conjugation (`ser` -> `son`):** The subject of the sentence is `Las chicas` (the girls), which corresponds to the third-person plural pronoun `ellas` (they). The verb `ser` must be conjugated to match this subject, becoming `son`.
2.  **Adjective Agreement (`inteligente` -> `inteligentes`):** The noun `chicas` is feminine and plural. The adjective `inteligente` ends in -e, so it does not change for gender. However, it must be made plural to agree with `chicas` by adding an -s, becoming `inteligentes`.

**Answer 6**
A correct sentence would be: "**Los edificios son modernos y nuevos.**"

**Reasoning:**
1.  **Noun:** The noun is `edificios`. This is a masculine plural noun.
2.  **Adjective `moderno`:** To agree with the masculine plural noun `edificios`, the masculine adjective `moderno` must be made plural by adding -s, resulting in `modernos`.
3.  **Adjective `nuevo`:** To agree with the masculine plural noun `edificios`, the masculine adjective `nuevo` must also be made plural by adding -s, resulting in `nuevos`.
4.  **Verb:** The verb `ser` is used to describe permanent characteristics. For a plural subject (`Los edificios`), the correct conjugation is `son`.
