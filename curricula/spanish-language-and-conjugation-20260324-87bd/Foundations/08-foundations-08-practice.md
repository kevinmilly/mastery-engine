## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Complete the following sentence with the correct form of 'estar'. The subject of the sentence is "the books" (*los libros*).

*Los libros _______ en la mesa.* (The books are on the table.)

**Exercise 2**
Your friend is crying and you want to ask them, "Are you sad?" How would you ask this question in Spanish using the informal 'you' (*tú*) and the verb 'estar'?

**Exercise 3**
A person says, *"Soy de Argentina, pero ahora estoy en México."* (I am from Argentina, but now I am in Mexico.)

Explain why the verb *ser* ('soy') is used for their origin and the verb *estar* ('estoy') is used for their current location.

**Exercise 4**
Consider the adjective *listo/a*.
- *Ana es lista.*
- *Ana está lista.*

These two sentences mean different things. One means "Ana is clever," and the other means "Ana is ready." Based on what you know about 'ser' and 'estar', which sentence means which? Explain your reasoning.

**Exercise 5**
You are on vacation with your sister. You want to write a short postcard to your parents that says: "The hotel is beautiful. We (female) are happy."

Translate this message into Spanish. You will need to use both 'ser' and 'estar' correctly and make sure your adjective for "happy" agrees in gender and number.

**Exercise 6**
Look around the room you are in right now. Write two complete, original sentences in Spanish that describe the location of two different objects. You must use a different form of 'estar' in each sentence (e.g., if you use *está* in the first sentence, use *están* in the second).

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The correct word is **están**.

**Reasoning:** The subject of the sentence is *los libros* (the books), which is a plural, third-person subject. It corresponds to the pronoun *ellos* (they). The correct conjugation of 'estar' for *ellos/ellas/ustedes* is *están*. The sentence describes the location of the books, which is a primary use of the verb 'estar'.

**Answer 2**
The correct question is: ***¿Estás triste?***

**Reasoning:** The question asks about a temporary emotional state (feeling sad), which requires the verb 'estar'. The subject is the informal 'you' (*tú*), so the correct conjugation is *estás*. In Spanish questions, the verb often comes before the subject.

**Answer 3**
*Ser* (*soy*) is used for origin because origin is considered a permanent characteristic or identity. Where you are from doesn't change. *Estar* (*estoy*) is used for the current location because location is considered a temporary state. The person is in Mexico *now*, but they might be somewhere else tomorrow.

**Answer 4**
- ***Ana es lista*** means "Ana is clever."
- ***Ana está lista*** means "Ana is ready."

**Reasoning:** 'Ser' is used for inherent characteristics and personality traits. Being clever (*lista*) is considered part of Ana's permanent character. 'Estar' is used for temporary conditions or states. Being ready (*lista*) is a temporary state; she might not have been ready an hour ago, and she won't be in this state forever.

**Answer 5**
The correct translation is: ***El hotel es bonito. Nosotras estamos contentas.***

**Reasoning:**
1.  **"The hotel is beautiful"** uses 'ser' (*es*) because you are describing a characteristic of the hotel.
2.  **"We (female) are happy"** uses 'estar' (*estamos*) because 'happy' is a temporary feeling or emotion.
3.  The subject "We (female)" requires the pronoun *nosotras*. The correct conjugation of 'estar' for *nosotras* is *estamos*.
4.  The adjective *contentas* must be feminine and plural to agree with the subject, *nosotras*.

**Answer 6**
Your answers will vary, but they must follow this structure. Here are two examples:

- **Sentence 1:** *La lámpara **está** al lado de la cama.* (The lamp is next to the bed.)
- **Sentence 2:** *Mis zapatos **están** debajo de la silla.* (My shoes are under the chair.)

**Reasoning:**
- Both sentences correctly use 'estar' to describe the location of an object.
- The first sentence uses the singular form *está* because the subject (*la lámpara*) is singular.
- The second sentence uses the plural form *están* because the subject (*mis zapatos*) is plural.
- Both sentences integrate prior knowledge of articles (*la*, *mis*), nouns, and prepositions to form a complete thought.
