## Capstone: Foundations — spanish language and conjugation

### The Scenario
You have just arrived at a university in Granada, Spain, for a semester-long language immersion program. After a long day of travel, you finally get the keys to your new dorm room. When you open the door, you meet your new roommate, an engineering student from Seville named Mateo. The room is simple: on one side is an empty bed and desk for you, and on the other is Mateo’s area.

Mateo is friendly and immediately starts a simple conversation. He smiles and says, “¡Hola! Yo soy Mateo. ¡Bienvenido/a a Granada!” On his desk, you notice a few specific items: a laptop and two large textbooks. On the wall above his bed, there is a poster of a famous landmark. He gestures around the room, clearly wanting to get to know you. This is your first opportunity to practice the Spanish you have learned.

### Your Tasks
Your goal is to write the Spanish sentences required for this initial conversation.

1.  **Introduce Yourself:** In two complete sentences, respond to Mateo's greeting. State your name and your country of origin.
2.  **Identify Your Luggage:** Mateo points to your bags on the floor and asks, "¿Qué es eso?" You have one large suitcase and one small backpack. Using indefinite articles, write one sentence that identifies both items and describes them with a simple adjective (e.g., big, small, new, old).
3.  **Describe the Location of Mateo's Things:** To keep the conversation going, you decide to comment on his side of the room. Write two separate sentences that state the location of his specific items: one sentence for the books on his desk and one sentence for the poster on the wall. Use definite articles.
4.  **Describe a Person:** Just then, Mateo's friend, Isabel, briefly pops her head in to say hello. Mateo introduces her, saying she is from Argentina and is very "inteligente y simpática" (intelligent and nice). After she leaves, write one complete sentence to practice describing her, using the correct subject pronoun and verb form based on what Mateo told you.
5.  **Give Your Opinion:** Mateo asks you, “¿Te gusta la habitación?” (Do you like the room?). Write two sentences in response. In the first sentence, describe a permanent characteristic of the room (e.g., it is big/small). In the second sentence, describe how you are feeling at this moment (e.g., you are tired/happy/excited).

### What Good Work Looks Like
*   A strong response demonstrates a clear understanding of when to use `ser` (for identity, origin, and inherent characteristics) versus `estar` (for location and temporary conditions/feelings).
*   All nouns, articles (definite and indefinite), and adjectives show correct agreement in both gender and number.
*   Sentences are constructed logically, using the correct subject pronouns and verb conjugations for the context of the conversation.
*   The choice between definite and indefinite articles is deliberate and correct, reflecting whether an object is being introduced for the first time or is a specific, identified item.
