## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Match the person or group on the left with the correct Spanish subject pronoun on the right.

1.  A group of female friends you are part of
2.  Your friend, Javier
3.  A group of male and female students you are talking about
4.  A police officer you are speaking to directly
5.  Yourself

a. yo
b. ellos
c. usted
d. nosotras
e. tú

**Exercise 2**
For each situation below, choose the single correct Spanish subject pronoun to replace the underlined person or group.

1. You are telling a story about what **your parents, María and Roberto,** did last weekend.
(yo / nosotros / ellos)

2. You are asking **your younger sister, Ana,** if she wants to go to the park.
(tú / ella / usted)

3. You and your friend are in Spain, and you are speaking directly to **a group of classmates your age.**
(nosotros / vosotros / ustedes)

**Exercise 3**
Imagine you are looking at a photo of your family: your mother (Ana), your father (Carlos), and your brother (David). If *you* (a male) are the one speaking, what subject pronoun would you use to refer to...

a) ...yourself and your brother, David?
b) ...your mother and father?
c) ...just your mother, Ana?

**Exercise 4**
You need to ask someone, "Are you from Argentina?" In Spanish, the pronoun for "you" changes based on your relationship with the person.

Which pronoun would you use for "you" (`tú` or `usted`) in the following two situations, and why?

*   **Situation A:** You are asking your new, 50-year-old boss this question during your first meeting.
*   **Situation B:** You are asking a fellow student who is your age this question while waiting for class to start.

**Exercise 5**
You have just introduced yourself to a group of people by saying, "Hola, soy de Canadá." (Hello, I'm from Canada.) Now you want to ask the group where they are from, by saying the equivalent of "And you all?".

How would you say "And you all?" in this context if you were in Madrid, Spain? How would you say it if you were in Mexico City, Mexico? Explain the reason for the difference.

**Exercise 6**
You are a university student at a conference. You are talking to another student, Sofia, who is also an attendee. Your famous professor, Dr. Ramirez, walks over and joins the conversation.

Which subject pronoun would you use to refer directly to...
a) ...Sofia, when the professor is not listening?
b) ...Dr. Ramirez, when you ask him a question?
c) ...both of them together, when you ask, "Are you both attending the next session?"?

Explain the social reasoning behind your choice for (c).

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The correct matches are:
-   1 → d (nosotras): "Nosotras" means "we" for an all-female group.
-   2 → e (tú): "Tú" is the informal "you" used for a friend.
-   3 → b (ellos): "Ellos" means "they" for a mixed-gender group.
-   4 → c (usted): "Usted" is the formal "you" used to show respect to a person in authority.
-   5 → a (yo): "Yo" means "I" or "myself."

**Answer 2**
1.  **ellos**: You are talking *about* your parents, who are a group of people not including you. "Ellos" is used for "they" (mixed-gender).
2.  **tú**: You are talking directly *to* your sister. The relationship is informal and familiar, so "tú" is appropriate.
3.  **vosotros**: You are speaking directly *to* a group of peers in Spain. "Vosotros" is the informal plural "you" used in most of Spain.

**Answer 3**
a) **nosotros**: This pronoun means "we." Since you (a male) are speaking about a group that includes yourself and another male, you use the masculine form "nosotros."
b) **ellos**: This pronoun means "they." You are speaking *about* your mother and father, a mixed-gender group, so you use "ellos."
c) **ella**: This pronoun means "she." You are speaking *about* only your mother, so you use the singular feminine pronoun "ella."

**Answer 4**
*   **Situation A:** You would use **usted**. The reason is that this is a formal relationship. You are speaking to your boss, who is older and in a position of authority. Using "usted" shows respect.
*   **Situation B:** You would use **tú**. The reason is that this is an informal, peer-to-peer relationship. You are speaking to a fellow student of a similar age, so the informal "tú" is appropriate.

**Answer 5**
*   In Madrid, Spain: You would ask, **"¿Y vosotros?"**
*   In Mexico City, Mexico: You would ask, **"¿Y ustedes?"**

**Reasoning:** "Vosotros/as" is the informal pronoun for "you all" and is primarily used in Spain. In Latin America, "ustedes" is used for both formal and informal situations when addressing a group of people.

**Answer 6**
a) To Sofia: You would use **tú**. She is a peer in an informal setting.
b) To Dr. Ramirez: You would use **usted**. He is a professor and a figure of authority, so you must show respect.
c) To both of them together: You would use **ustedes**.

**Reasoning for (c):** When addressing a group that includes at least one person you would call "usted" (Dr. Ramirez), the entire group is addressed with the formal plural "ustedes" out of respect. The formal pronoun "overrides" the informal one in a mixed group.
