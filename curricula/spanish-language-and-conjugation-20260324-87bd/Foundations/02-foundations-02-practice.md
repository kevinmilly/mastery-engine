## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
You are starting a video call with a colleague in Madrid at 3:00 PM local time. Which of the three main time-based greetings (`Buenos días`, `Buenas tardes`, `Buenas noches`) is the most appropriate to use, and why?

**Exercise 2**
Below is a short conversation where two people meet. Fill in the blank for the second person's response, using the most common and logical phrase from the lesson.

*   **Marisol:** ¡Hola! Me llamo Marisol. Mucho gusto.
*   **Leo:** Me llamo Leo. _______________.

**Exercise 3**
You are introduced to a new classmate named Elena. After you both say your names, she says, "Encantada." Later, you are introduced to another classmate, David. After you both say your names, he says, "Encantado." Why do their responses, which both mean "Delighted," end differently?

**Exercise 4**
You are leaving a one-time business meeting with a client you don't expect to see again soon. You are also leaving your local library, where you plan to return the next day. Which farewell, `Adiós` or `Hasta luego`, is better suited for each situation? Justify your choices.

**Exercise 5**
Your name is "Chad." Based on your prior lesson on Spanish Alphabet & Pronunciation, how would the letters in your name likely be pronounced by a native Spanish speaker? Write down the Spanish sounds for C-H-A-D and explain how it differs from the English pronunciation.

**Exercise 6**
Imagine you are at a casual party. You see someone you don't know, walk up to them, and want to start a conversation. Using only phrases from this lesson, write the three-line exchange that would allow you to greet them, give your name, and ask for theirs.

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The most appropriate greeting is `Buenas tardes`.

**Reasoning:**
*   `Buenos días` is used for the morning, typically until noon or 1:00 PM.
*   `Buenas tardes` is used for the afternoon and early evening, from about 1:00 PM until sunset (around 7-8 PM). 3:00 PM falls squarely in this range.
*   `Buenas noches` is used at night, after it gets dark.

**Answer 2**
The blank should be filled with `Igualmente`.

**Reasoning:**
Marisol has just said "Mucho gusto" (Nice to meet you). The standard, reciprocal response to this is "Igualmente," which means "Likewise" or "Same here." While saying "Mucho gusto" back is also acceptable, "Igualmente" is a common and efficient way to return the sentiment.

**Answer 3**
Their responses end differently to match their gender.

**Reasoning:**
In Spanish, many adjectives change their ending to agree with the gender of the noun they describe.
*   Elena says "Encantad**a**" because she is female. The "-a" ending is feminine.
*   David says "Encantad**o**" because he is male. The "-o" ending is masculine.
This is an important concept in Spanish grammar that appears even in simple introductory phrases.

**Answer 4**
*   **For the client:** `Adiós` is more suitable.
*   **For the library:** `Hasta luego` is more suitable.

**Reasoning:**
`Adiós` is a more formal and final-sounding farewell. It can mean "goodbye" in a permanent sense, or simply a more definite end to an interaction. It is appropriate for the client you don't expect to see again.
`Hasta luego` literally means "Until later." It implies that you expect to see the person again, making it the perfect choice for leaving a place like the library that you plan to visit again soon.

**Answer 5**
A Spanish speaker would likely pronounce the name "Chad" using the sounds for "che," "a," and "de."

**Reasoning:**
This integrates knowledge of Spanish phonetics with the introduction.
*   **C-H:** In Spanish, "ch" is its own distinct sound, pronounced like the "ch" in the English word "church."
*   **A:** The Spanish "a" is a pure, open vowel sound, like the "a" in "father."
*   **D:** The Spanish "d" is softer than in English. The tongue touches the back of the front teeth, making a sound closer to "th" in the English word "the."
Therefore, the English name "Chad" would sound phonetically like "Chahd" to a Spanish speaker, applying Spanish pronunciation rules.

**Answer 6**
A logical three-line exchange would be:

*   **You:** ¡Hola! Me llamo [Your Name].
*   **Them:** ¡Hola! Me llamo [Their Name]. Mucho gusto.
*   **You:** ¿Cómo te llamas? *or* Igualmente.

**Reasoning:**
This sequence synthesizes the lesson's components into a realistic, self-initiated conversation.
1.  **Line 1 (You):** You start with a simple greeting (`¡Hola!`) and immediately offer your name (`Me llamo...`). This is a friendly and direct opening.
2.  **Line 2 (Them):** A natural response is for them to return the greeting and offer their name and a pleasantry in return.
3.  **Line 3 (You):** If they hadn't offered their name in line 2, your logical next step would be to ask for it (`¿Cómo te llamas?`). Since they did in this example, the proper follow-up to their "Mucho gusto" is "Igualmente." This demonstrates the ability to build a conversation flow.
