## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Complete the following sentence by conjugating the verb in parentheses in the preterite tense.

*Ayer, yo ________ (comprar) un libro nuevo.*

**Exercise 2**
Complete the following sentence by conjugating the verb in parentheses in the preterite tense.

*La semana pasada, Marta y Lucía ________ (aprender) a bailar salsa.*

**Exercise 3**
A student wrote the sentence: "El año pasado, tú *viajastes* a México." This is incorrect. Identify the error, provide the correct conjugation, and explain why this specific mistake is common for English speakers learning Spanish.

**Exercise 4**
You are describing what your parents did last night. Using the verb *decidir*, write a complete Spanish sentence that says they decided to watch a movie. Explain why you chose the specific verb ending.

**Exercise 5**
Complete the following short narrative by conjugating the verbs in parentheses in the preterite tense. The entire story is told from the perspective of a single narrator, "I".

*El sábado, mi amigo y yo (caminar) al centro. Nosotros (entrar) en una tienda de música. Yo (mirar) los discos y mi amigo (encontrar) una guitarra perfecta. Al final, él no (comprar) la guitarra, pero yo (pagar) por un disco de jazz.*

**Exercise 6**
Imagine you meet a friend who just returned from a trip to Peru. Formulate a logical question in Spanish asking them if they "lived" (*vivir*) in Lima during their trip. Then, provide a plausible negative response from their perspective, explaining that they "visited" (*visitar*) several cities instead. Both sentences must use the preterite tense.

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
**Sentence:** *Ayer, yo **compré** un libro nuevo.*

**Reasoning:** The subject is *yo* (I). The verb is *comprar*, an -ar verb. The regular preterite ending for the *yo* form of -ar verbs is **-é**.

**Answer 2**
**Sentence:** *La semana pasada, Marta y Lucía **aprendieron** a bailar salsa.*

**Reasoning:** The subject is "Marta y Lucía," which corresponds to the pronoun *ellas* (they). The verb is *aprender*, an -er verb. The regular preterite ending for the *ellos/ellas/ustedes* form of -er and -ir verbs is **-ieron**.

**Answer 3**
**Corrected Sentence:** "El año pasado, tú **viajaste** a México."

**Reasoning:** The error is the extra "s" at the end of the verb. The correct preterite conjugation for the *tú* form of -ar verbs is the ending **-aste**, not "-astes". This is a common error due to over-applying a rule from the present tense, where the *tú* form almost always ends in "s" (e.g., *viajas*, *comes*, *vives*). In the preterite, this "s" is dropped.

**Answer 4**
**Sentence:** *Anoche, mis padres decidieron ver una película.*

**Reasoning:** The subject is "mis padres" (my parents), which corresponds to the pronoun *ellos* (they). The verb is *decidir*, an -ir verb. The correct preterite ending for the *ellos/ellas/ustedes* form of regular -ir verbs is **-ieron**.

**Answer 5**
**Completed Narrative:**
*El sábado, mi amigo y yo **caminamos** al centro. Nosotros **entramos** en una tienda de música. Yo **miré** los discos y mi amigo **encontró** una guitarra perfecta. Al final, él no **compró** la guitarra, pero yo **pagué** por un disco de jazz.*

**Reasoning:**
*   **caminamos, entramos:** The subject is "mi amigo y yo," which is *nosotros*. The -ar verb ending is **-amos**.
*   **miré, pagué:** The subject is *yo*. The -ar verb ending is **-é**.
*   **encontró, compró:** The subject is "mi amigo," which is *él*. The -ar verb ending is **-ó**.

**Answer 6**
**Question:** *¿Viviste tú en Lima durante tu viaje?*
**Response:** *No, yo no viví en Lima. Yo visité varias ciudades.*

**Reasoning:**
*   The question must be directed at *tú* (you, informal). The verb *vivir* is an -ir verb, so its *tú* form in the preterite takes the **-iste** ending.
*   The response must be from the *yo* (I) perspective. The verb *vivir* takes the **-í** ending for *yo*. The second verb, *visitar*, is an -ar verb, so it takes the **-é** ending for *yo*. This demonstrates the ability to form a conversational exchange using the correct verb forms for different subjects and verb types (-ar and -ir).
