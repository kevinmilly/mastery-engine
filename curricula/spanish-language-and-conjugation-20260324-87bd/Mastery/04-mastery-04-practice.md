## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
While a student was studying, their roommate suddenly entered the room. Choose the correct tense (preterite or imperfect) for the verbs in parentheses to accurately describe this scene in Spanish. Explain your reasoning for each choice.

"Mientras yo ______ (estudiar), mi compañero de cuarto ______ (entrar) de repente."

**Exercise 2**
Both sentences below describe knowing someone, but the meaning is different in each. Explain the nuance of meaning that the choice between the preterite and imperfect of the verb *saber* creates in these two contexts.

Sentence A: "Yo no *sabía* que la tienda cerraba a las cinco."
Sentence B: "En ese momento, yo *supe* que había cometido un error."

**Exercise 3**
Imagine you are telling a story about your childhood. You want to describe your elementary school. Which tense would you primarily use to describe what the school building was like, what your teacher was like, and what you and your friends used to do during recess? Provide a one-sentence example for each of the three descriptions (the school, the teacher, and the recess activities) and justify why that tense is the appropriate choice for setting this kind of scene.

**Exercise 4**
Read the following short police report narrative. Fill in the blanks with the correct preterite or imperfect form of the verbs in parentheses. Then, explain why the two different tenses are both necessary to correctly report the sequence of events.

"Eran las diez de la noche. ______ (llover) con fuerza y pocas personas ______ (estar) en la calle. El detective ______ (caminar) cerca del muelle cuando ______ (oír) un grito. ______ (Correr) hacia el sonido y ______ (encontrar) una puerta abierta."

**Exercise 5**
Your friend sent you the following text message with a list of things that happened yesterday. Combine this information into a single, cohesive Spanish sentence that uses both the preterite and imperfect to show the relationship between the background situation and the main event.

- Background situation: I was waiting for the bus.
- Main event: I saw my old history teacher.

**Exercise 6**
A fellow student wrote the following paragraph to describe a memorable concert. Their use of tenses is unnatural and makes the story confusing. Identify two verbs that are incorrectly conjugated in the imperfect and should be in the preterite. Rewrite them correctly and explain why the preterite is necessary to make the sequence of events clear.

"El concierto *era* anoche. La banda *tocaba* su primera canción cuando las luces se *apagaban*. El cantante *pedía* a todos que usaran las linternas de sus teléfonos. *Era* un momento mágico."

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The correct sentence is: "Mientras yo **estudiaba** (imperfect), mi compañero de cuarto **entró** (preterite) de repente."

**Reasoning:**
- **estudiaba (imperfect):** The action of studying was ongoing and in progress, serving as the background scene. The imperfect is used for actions without a specified end point that set the stage for another event.
- **entró (preterite):** The roommate's entrance was a specific, single, completed action that interrupted the ongoing action of studying. The preterite is used for these kinds of interrupting actions that have a clear beginning and end.

**Answer 2**
The choice of tense for the verb *saber* changes its meaning from a state of knowing to the specific event of finding out or realizing.

**Reasoning:**
- **Sentence A: "Yo no sabía..." (imperfect):** The imperfect *sabía* describes a state of knowledge (or lack thereof) over an unspecified period of time. It's equivalent to "I wasn't aware" or "I didn't know." It was the background state of mind.
- **Sentence B: "En ese momento, yo supe..." (preterite):** The preterite *supe* describes the specific moment of realization or discovery. It's equivalent to "I found out" or "I realized." It is treated as a completed event at a specific point in time ("en ese momento").

**Answer 3**
To describe a recurring or continuous state in the past, such as setting a scene from childhood, the imperfect tense is the primary choice.

**Reasoning:**
The imperfect is used for descriptions of people, places, and things in the past, as well as for habitual actions. These descriptions do not have a specific start or end point; they represent the "way things were."

- **The school:** "La escuela **era** grande y amarilla." (The imperfect *era* is used to describe the state or characteristics of the school building.)
- **The teacher:** "Mi maestra **tenía** el pelo largo y siempre **sonreía**." (The imperfect *tenía* and *sonreía* describe the teacher's appearance and habitual demeanor.)
- **Recess activities:** "Durante el recreo, mis amigos y yo siempre **jugábamos** al fútbol." (The imperfect *jugábamos* describes a habitual, repeated action that used to happen.)

**Answer 4**
The completed paragraph is: "Eran las diez de la noche. **Llovía** con fuerza y pocas personas **estaban** en la calle. El detective **caminaba** cerca del muelle cuando **oyó** un grito. **Corrió** hacia el sonido y **encontró** una puerta abierta."

**Reasoning:**
Both tenses are necessary to distinguish background information from the main plot.
- The imperfect (**llovía**, **estaban**, **caminaba**) is used to set the scene and describe the ongoing conditions: the time, the weather, what people were doing, and what the detective was doing. These actions were in progress and formed the backdrop.
- The preterite (**oyó**, **corrió**, **encontró**) is used to narrate the sequence of specific, completed events that advance the story. The detective heard a cry (event 1), ran toward it (event 2), and found a door (event 3). These are the key actions of the plot.

**Answer 5**
A correct and natural-sounding sentence would be: "**Esperaba** el autobús cuando **vi** a mi antiguo profesor de historia."

**Reasoning:**
This structure effectively uses the two tenses to show a relationship between actions.
- **Esperaba (imperfect):** This describes the ongoing background action. "Waiting" was the situation you were in, without a specific focus on its completion.
- **vi (preterite):** This describes the specific, completed event that happened *while* you were in that state of waiting. Seeing the teacher was a momentary action that punctuated the background scene.

**Answer 6**
The two verbs that are used incorrectly are "tocaba" and "apagaban". The paragraph should be corrected to use the preterite for these actions.

**Corrected verbs:**
1.  ...La banda **tocó**...
2.  ...cuando las luces se **apagaron**.

**Reasoning:**
The original paragraph uses the imperfect for actions that are clearly part of a sequence of main events.
1.  **tocó (preterite):** The band playing its first song is a specific, completed event that started the concert's main action. Using *tocaba* (imperfect) would imply they were in the middle of playing for an undefined time, which doesn't fit the narrative of a sequence. The story is about *what happened*, so the preterite is needed.
2.  **apagaron (preterite):** The lights going out is a sudden, specific event that interrupted the song. This is a classic use case for the preterite as an interrupting action. Using *apagaban* (imperfect) would suggest the lights were flickering or fading out over a period, which is less likely and makes the narrative less punchy. The preterite shows it happened at a distinct moment.
