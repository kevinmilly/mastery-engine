## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Complete the sentence with the correct preterite form of the verb in parentheses. Then, explain why the verb stem changes for this conjugation.

`¿Qué (hacer) ______________ tú para tu cumpleaños el año pasado?`

**Exercise 2**
Both sentences below use the same conjugated verb form, `fue`. Based on the context of each sentence, identify whether `fue` comes from the verb `ser` or `ir` and explain your reasoning.

1.  `Mi primer coche fue un modelo muy viejo, pero funcionaba bien.`
2.  `Marta fue a la biblioteca para estudiar para su examen de historia.`

**Exercise 3**
A fellow student has written the following sentence, attempting to use the verb `ver` in the preterite: `Yo ví una película increíble anoche.`

There is a common spelling mistake in their conjugation. Identify the error, provide the correct spelling, and explain the rule that applies here.

**Exercise 4**
Imagine you are describing a past event where you gave a presentation. Which of the following sentences is the most appropriate and why? Analyze the subtle difference in meaning between using the verb `hacer` versus `dar`.

A) `Yo hice una presentación sobre la historia de México.`
B) `Yo di una presentación sobre la historia de México.`

**Exercise 5**
Complete the following short narrative by conjugating the verbs in parentheses in the preterite tense. Be prepared to use both the irregular verbs from this lesson and regular verbs from the previous lesson.

`Ayer, mi amigo y yo (ir) ______________ al centro comercial. Nosotros (ver) ______________ una camisa que nos gustó mucho, pero no la (comprar) ______________. Después, le (dar) ______________ a mi madre el regalo que habíamos elegido para ella. Ella (abrir) ______________ la caja y le encantó. ¡(Ser) ______________ un día muy bueno!`

**Exercise 6**
You are recounting a surprising event to a friend. Write a short paragraph (2-4 sentences) describing what happened. Your paragraph must correctly use the preterite forms of `ver`, `hacer`, and `ser`/`ir`.

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The correct sentence is: `¿Qué **hiciste** tú para tu cumpleaños el año pasado?`

**Reasoning:**
The verb `hacer` is irregular in the preterite. The stem changes from `hac-` to `hic-` in most forms (`hizo` for él/ella/usted). This is simply an irregularity that must be memorized. The `tú` form uses the irregular stem `hic-` and adds the standard `-iste` ending for the second-person singular.

**Answer 2**
1.  In `Mi primer coche fue un modelo muy viejo...`, **`fue` comes from `ser`** (to be). The sentence is describing the characteristics or identity of the car ("it was a very old model"). The verb `ser` is used for descriptions, origins, and inherent qualities.
2.  In `Marta fue a la biblioteca...`, **`fue` comes from `ir`** (to go). The sentence is describing an action of movement to a destination ("Marta went to the library"). The preposition `a` following the verb is a strong indicator of movement, signaling the use of `ir`.

**Answer 3**
The error is the accent mark on `ví`. The correct spelling is `vi`.

**Reasoning:**
The preterite forms of `ver` (vi, viste, vio, vimos, visteis, vieron) and `dar` (di, diste, dio, dimos, disteis, dieron) do not have accent marks. According to Spanish spelling rules, single-syllable words generally do not carry a written accent. The first-person (`yo`) and third-person (`él/ella/usted`) forms of these verbs (`vi`, `vio`, `di`, `dio`) are monosyllabic and therefore follow this rule. This is an exception to the standard preterite accent patterns seen in regular verbs.

**Answer 4**
Both sentences are grammatically correct, but sentence B (`Yo di una presentación...`) is more common and natural in most Spanish-speaking regions.

**Reasoning:**
-   `Hacer una presentación` literally means "to make a presentation," which could imply the entire process of creating the slides and content. While understandable, it's less common for describing the act of delivering it.
-   `Dar una presentación` is a common collocation that means "to give a presentation." It specifically refers to the act of speaking and presenting the information to an audience. The verb `dar` is often used in this idiomatic way with nouns like `presentación`, `discurso` (speech), or `clase` (class). Therefore, B is the more appropriate choice for describing the event of delivering the presentation.

**Answer 5**
The completed narrative is:

`Ayer, mi amigo y yo **fuimos** al centro comercial. Nosotros **vimos** una camisa que nos gustó mucho, pero no la **compramos**. Después, le **dimos** a mi madre el regalo que habíamos elegido para ella. Ella **abrió** la caja y le encantó. ¡**Fue** un día muy bueno!`

**Reasoning:**
-   `fuimos` (`ir`): Irregular preterite, `nosotros` form.
-   `vimos` (`ver`): Irregular preterite, `nosotros` form. Note the lack of an accent.
-   `compramos` (`comprar`): Regular -ar preterite verb. The `nosotros` form is the same in the present and preterite; context indicates the past.
-   `dimos` (`dar`): Irregular preterite, `nosotros` form.
-   `abrió` (`abrir`): Regular -ir preterite verb, `ella` form, with an accent on the `ó`.
-   `fue` (`ser`): Irregular preterite, `él/ella/usted` form, used here to describe the day.

**Answer 6**
(Example answer)
`Ayer, yo fui al parque para leer. De repente, vi algo extraño en el cielo. Hizo un ruido muy fuerte y desapareció rápidamente. ¡Fue una experiencia increíble!`

**Reasoning:**
This paragraph successfully uses three of the required irregular verbs in the preterite:
-   `fui` (from `ir`): Describes the action of going to a place (`al parque`).
-   `vi` (from `ver`): Describes the action of seeing something (`algo extraño`).
-   `Hizo` (from `hacer`): Describes the action of making a sound (`un ruido`).
-   `Fue` (from `ser`): Describes the nature of the experience (`una experiencia increíble`).
The conjugations are correct for their respective subjects (`yo`, `algo extraño`/it, `la experiencia`/it), and they are integrated into a coherent narrative, demonstrating synthesis of the concepts.
