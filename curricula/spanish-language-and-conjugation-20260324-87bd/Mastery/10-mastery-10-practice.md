## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Fill in the blanks with either 'por' or 'para'. Then, in one sentence, explain the grammatical reason for each choice.

"No pudimos ir a la playa ___ la lluvia, así que cambiamos nuestros planes ___ visitar el museo."

**Exercise 2**
Choose the correct preposition ('por' or 'para') for each blank in the following sentence. Justify each of your three choices by identifying the specific rule of usage (e.g., recipient, exchange, means).

"Compré estas flores ___ mi madre, pagué quince dólares ___ ellas, y las envié ___ correo."

**Exercise 3**
The following two sentences are grammatically correct but have very different meanings. Explain the difference in the relationship between the speaker and "la empresa" (the company) in each case.

Sentence A: "Hice el proyecto para la empresa."
Sentence B: "Hice el proyecto por la empresa."

**Exercise 4**
A manager gives her team the following instructions for a new assignment. Analyze the two statements and explain the precise meaning of each. What is the deadline for the assignment, and for how long is the team expected to focus on it?

Statement 1: "Este es el único proyecto en el que trabajaremos por las próximas dos semanas."
Statement 2: "La presentación final debe estar lista para el último día del mes."

**Exercise 5**
Your roommate is sick and can't go to the pharmacy. You decide to go for them. You want to say, "I'm going to the pharmacy so that you can rest."

Translate this sentence into Spanish, making sure to use the correct preposition and verb mood. Then, explain why the structure 'para que' is used here and why it triggers the subjunctive mood.

**Exercise 6**
A friend asks why you didn't finish your homework last night (`¿Por qué no terminaste la tarea anoche?`). Construct a two-sentence response in Spanish that explains the situation by accurately incorporating the following four elements:

1.  The cause of the problem: a power outage (un apagón).
2.  The purpose of an action you took: to find a flashlight.
3.  The ongoing state of the house (it was very dark) using the imperfect tense.
4.  A completed action (you called the power company) using the preterite tense.

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
"No pudimos ir a la playa **por** la lluvia, así que cambiamos nuestros planes **para** visitar el museo."

**Reasoning:** 'Por' is used to indicate the reason or cause for something ("because of the rain"). 'Para' is used to express purpose or an objective ("in order to visit the museum").

**Answer 2**
"Compré estas flores **para** mi madre, pagué quince dólares **por** ellas, y las envié **por** correo."

**Reasoning:**
1.  **para mi madre:** 'Para' is used to indicate the recipient or intended destination of an object.
2.  **por ellas:** 'Por' is used to indicate an exchange (money for flowers).
3.  **por correo:** 'Por' is used to indicate the means or manner by which something is done.

**Answer 3**
**Reasoning:** The choice of preposition changes the speaker's role and motivation.

*   **Sentence A ("Hice el proyecto para la empresa.")** uses 'para' to indicate purpose or destination. This means the project was made *for* the company, as an employee or a contractor. The company is the recipient of the work. This is the most common and neutral way to state that you did a project as part of your job.
*   **Sentence B ("Hice el proyecto por la empresa.")** uses 'por' to indicate motivation, reason, or acting on behalf of something. This implies a deeper, more personal reason. The speaker might mean they did it "for the good of the company" during a crisis, or they were substituting for a colleague, or they felt a sense of duty. It suggests the action was taken out of loyalty or necessity, not just as a standard work assignment.

**Answer 4**
**Reasoning:** The manager is using 'por' and 'para' to distinguish between duration and a deadline.

*   **Statement 1 ("...trabajaremos por las próximas dos semanas.")** uses 'por' to express a duration of time. This means the team's work on the project will last for a period of two weeks.
*   **Statement 2 ("...lista para el último día del mes.")** uses 'para' to set a specific deadline. The project must be completed on or before the last day of the month. It does not matter how long the work takes, as long as it is finished by that point in time.

**Answer 5**
**Spanish Sentence:** "Voy a la farmacia para que (tú) descanses."

**Reasoning:**
The structure 'para que' is used because it expresses the purpose of an action when there is a change in subject. The first subject ("yo") is going to the pharmacy with the intention of causing the second subject ("tú") to be able to rest. When 'para' is followed by 'que' and a change of subject, it triggers the use of the subjunctive mood ('descanses' instead of the indicative 'descansas'). This is because the outcome (you resting) is the intended purpose or hope of the first person's action, not a factual, completed event.

**Answer 6**
**Sample Response:** "No la terminé **por** un apagón que hubo en mi barrio. Toda la casa estaba muy oscura, así que busqué **para** encontrar una linterna y llamé a la compañía eléctrica."

**Reasoning:**
This response correctly synthesizes the required elements:
1.  **por un apagón:** 'Por' is used to state the cause or reason for not finishing the homework.
2.  **para encontrar una linterna:** 'Para' is used to express the purpose or goal of the search.
3.  **estaba muy oscura:** The imperfect tense ('estaba') is used to describe the ongoing state or condition of the house.
4.  **llamé:** The preterite tense ('llamé') is used to describe a single, completed action that occurred in the past.
