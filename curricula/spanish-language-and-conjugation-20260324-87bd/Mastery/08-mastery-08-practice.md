## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Your friend is daydreaming about what they would do if they were a famous musician. Complete their sentences by conjugating the verb in parentheses into the simple conditional tense for the subject "yo".
a) (Tener) _______ un estudio de grabación en mi casa.
b) (Componer) _______ canciones todos los días.

**Exercise 2**
You are at a hotel reception desk. Transform your direct needs into polite requests using the conditional tense. You may need to add words like *poder* or *gustar*.
a) Direct: "Necesito otra toalla." (I need another towel.)
b) Direct: "Quiero saber a qué hora es el desayuno." (I want to know what time breakfast is.)

**Exercise 3**
A friend is talking about their plans. In one sentence, they use the future tense, and in another, the conditional. Explain the difference in meaning between the two sentences based on the choice of tense.
Sentence A: "El próximo año, **haré** un viaje a Perú."
Sentence B: "Si tuviera el dinero, **haría** un viaje a Perú."

**Exercise 4**
Someone asks you why your coworker, Carlos, wasn't at the morning meeting ("¿Dónde estaba Carlos esta mañana?"). You aren't certain, but you have a strong guess. Choose the verb that best expresses your guess from the options below, and explain why the conditional tense is the correct choice to express probability or conjecture about a past event.

Options:
a) Estuvo enfermo. (He was sick - Preterite)
b) Estaba enfermo. (He was sick - Imperfect)
c) Estaría enfermo. (He was probably sick / I guess he was sick - Conditional)

**Exercise 5**
Many irregular verbs in the conditional tense share the same irregular stem as in the future tense. Transform the following statements of future certainty into hypothetical statements using the conditional. This will require changing the verb from the future to the conditional tense.

a) Future Certainty: "Nosotros **saldremos** a las ocho." (We will leave at eight.)
b) Hypothetical: "Si no lloviera, nosotros ________ a las ocho."

c) Future Certainty: "Yo te **diré** la verdad." (I will tell you the truth.)
d) Hypothetical: "En tu lugar, yo le ________ la verdad."

**Exercise 6**
You are emailing a professor to ask for an extension on an assignment due tomorrow. Write a short, polite email (2-3 sentences) in Spanish. Your email must:
1. Briefly state your situation (e.g., you have been sick, you have a family emergency).
2. Use the conditional tense to politely ask if you *could* submit the assignment late.
3. Use the conditional tense again to state what you *would like* to do (e.g., propose a new deadline).

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
a) **Tendría** un estudio de grabación en mi casa.
b) **Compondría** canciones todos los días.

**Reasoning:** The exercise describes a hypothetical, "daydreaming" scenario, which is a primary use of the conditional tense.
a) *Tener* is an irregular verb. Its conditional stem is `tendr-`. The first-person singular ("yo") ending for the conditional is `-ía`. So, `tendr-` + `-ía` = `tendría`.
b) *Componer* is a regular -er verb. The conditional is formed by taking the full infinitive (`componer`) and adding the first-person singular ending `-ía`. So, `componer` + `-ía` = `compondría`.

**Answer 2**
a) ¿**Podría** traerme otra toalla, por favor? (Could you bring me another towel, please?)
b) Me **gustaría** saber a qué hora es el desayuno. (I would like to know what time breakfast is.)

**Reasoning:** The conditional tense is used to soften direct requests and make them more polite.
a) Instead of the direct "Necesito," using the verb *poder* (to be able) in the conditional (*podría*) turns the demand into a polite question.
b) Instead of the direct "Quiero," using the verb *gustar* (to like) in the conditional (*me gustaría*) expresses a desire or preference politely, which is a very common construction in Spanish.

**Answer 3**
Sentence A ("haré") uses the future tense, which indicates a firm plan or certainty. The speaker fully intends to travel to Peru next year.
Sentence B ("haría") uses the conditional tense. The phrase "Si tuviera el dinero" (If I had the money) establishes a hypothetical condition. The speaker is saying what they *would do* if that condition were met. It is not a definite plan; it is a dream or a possibility that depends on something else.

**Reasoning:** The key distinction is between certainty (future) and hypothesis (conditional). The future tense talks about what *will* happen, while the conditional talks about what *would* happen under certain, often unreal, circumstances.

**Answer 4**
The correct choice is (c) **Estaría enfermo.**

**Reasoning:** While the preterite (a) and imperfect (b) both state that Carlos *was* sick as a fact, the conditional (c) is used here to express conjecture or probability about a past action. The speaker doesn't know for sure why Carlos was absent, but is making an educated guess. It is the English equivalent of saying "He must have been sick," "He was probably sick," or "I'd imagine he was sick." This is a more nuanced use of the conditional beyond simple "if...then" scenarios.

**Answer 5**
b) Si no lloviera, nosotros **saldríamos** a las ocho.
d) En tu lugar, yo le **diría** la verdad.

**Reasoning:** This exercise shows the direct link between the irregular stems of the future and conditional tenses.
a/b) The future of *salir* is *saldremos*. The irregular stem is `saldr-`. To form the conditional for "nosotros", we use this same stem and add the conditional ending `-íamos`, resulting in `saldríamos`.
c/d) The future of *decir* is *diré*. The irregular stem is `dir-`. To form the conditional for "yo", we use this same stem and add the conditional ending `-ía`, resulting in `diría`.

**Answer 6**
*Example Answer (answers may vary but should follow the structure):*

Estimada Profesora López,

Le escribo porque he estado enfermo/a estos últimos días. ¿**Sería** posible entregar el trabajo un poco más tarde? Me **gustaría** proponer entregarlo este viernes si está de acuerdo.

Muchas gracias,
[Your Name]

**Reasoning:** This response correctly applies the conditional tense in a realistic scenario.
1.  "¿**Sería** posible...?" uses the conditional of *ser* to make a very polite and indirect request, which is more appropriate than asking directly "¿Es posible?".
2.  "Me **gustaría** proponer..." uses the common conditional construction of *gustar* to politely state a preference or suggestion, rather than making a demand ("Quiero proponer...").
The answer fulfills all requirements of the prompt by blending the grammatical concept into a practical, nuanced piece of communication.
