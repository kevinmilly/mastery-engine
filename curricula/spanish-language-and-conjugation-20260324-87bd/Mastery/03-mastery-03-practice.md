## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
When describing a habitual action in the past, the imperfect tense is used. Complete the following sentence by conjugating the verb `caminar` in the imperfect tense for the subject `nosotros`.

"Cada mañana, mi hermano y yo (caminar) a la escuela juntos."

**Exercise 2**
The verb `ver` (to see/to watch) is one of the three common irregular verbs in the imperfect. Given this context, conjugate `ver` to complete the sentence below describing a family's past routine.

"Durante las vacaciones de invierno, mi familia siempre (ver) películas clásicas por la noche."

**Exercise 3**
Analyze the following short paragraph, which describes a university student's routine. Identify the one verb that is conjugated incorrectly in the imperfect tense and provide the correct form.

"Yo estudiaba en la biblioteca todos los días. A menudo, mis amigos venían a estudiar conmigo. Nosotros leía en silencio por horas, y a veces tomábamos un descanso para tomar café. La vida era simple."

**Exercise 4**
A friend is telling you about the setting of a festival they used to attend every year. Translate the following English description into Spanish, ensuring you use the imperfect tense to describe the ongoing sights and sounds of the scene.

"Many people were dancing, and the music was playing loudly."

**Exercise 5**
This sentence describes an ongoing past action that was interrupted by a sudden event. To narrate this, you must use both the imperfect and the preterite tenses. Fill in the blanks by correctly conjugating `cocinar` and `llamar` in the appropriate past tense.

"Mi madre (cocinar) la cena cuando mi tía (llamar) por teléfono."

**Exercise 6**
Imagine you are telling a story about a day at the park when you were a child. Write two sentences in Spanish that set the scene and describe a specific event that happened. Your response must use:
1.  At least one verb in the imperfect tense to describe the background situation (e.g., the weather, what people were doing).
2.  At least one verb in the preterite tense to describe a main, completed action.

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The correct conjugation is **caminábamos**.

**Reasoning:** The verb is `caminar`, a regular -ar verb. The imperfect ending for `nosotros` with -ar verbs is `-ábamos`. The stem is `camin-`. Combining them gives `caminábamos`. The sentence describes a habitual action ("Cada mañana"), which is a key use case for the imperfect tense.

**Answer 2**
The correct conjugation is **veía**.

**Reasoning:** The verb is `ver`. Although it is irregular in the imperfect, its conjugation pattern is simple. For the subject `mi familia` (which corresponds to `ella` or the third-person singular), the correct form is `veía`. The sentence describes a repeated family routine ("siempre"), signaling the use of the imperfect.

**Answer 3**
The incorrect verb is `leía`. The correct form is **leíamos**.

**Reasoning:** The subject of the verb is `Nosotros` (we). The verb `leer` is a regular -er verb in the imperfect. The correct imperfect ending for `nosotros` with -er verbs is `-íamos`. The form `leía` would be used for `yo` or `él/ella/usted`, which does not agree with the subject `Nosotros`.

**Answer 4**
A correct translation is: **Mucha gente bailaba y la música sonaba fuertemente.**

**Reasoning:**
-   "Many people were dancing" describes an ongoing, continuous action in the past with no specific end point. This requires the imperfect. The verb is `bailar`, and the subject `mucha gente` (third-person plural) conjugates to `bailaban`.
-   "the music was playing" is background description, another primary use of the imperfect. The verb is `sonar`, and the subject `la música` (third-person singular) conjugates to `sonaba`.
*(Note: Minor variations like "la gente bailaba" are also acceptable.)*

**Answer 5**
Mi madre **cocinaba** la cena cuando mi tía **llamó** por teléfono.

**Reasoning:**
1.  `cocinaba` (imperfect): This action, "my mother was cooking," was in progress and set the background for the story. The imperfect is used for ongoing past actions.
2.  `llamó` (preterite): This action, "my aunt called," was a single, completed event that interrupted the ongoing action of cooking. The preterite is used for specific, completed actions that move the story forward.

**Answer 6**
*Sample Answer:* **Era un día hermoso y el sol brillaba. De repente, mi perro se escapó y corrió por todo el parque.**

**Reasoning:**
-   **Imperfect:** `Era` (from `ser`) and `brillaba` (from `brillar`) are used to describe the background conditions—the state of the day and the weather. They set the scene without referring to a specific, completed event.
-   **Preterite:** `se escapó` (from `escaparse`) and `corrió` (from `correr`) describe two specific, sequential actions that happened and were completed. They are the main events of the story. Your answer should demonstrate a similar distinction between background description (imperfect) and plot-driving action (preterite).
