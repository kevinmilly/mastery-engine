## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Your friend is making a to-do list for their trip to Mexico City next month. Complete their list by conjugating the verbs in parentheses into the future simple tense for the subject "yo".

1.  (visitar) el Palacio de Bellas Artes.
2.  (poder) ver las pirámides de Teotihuacán.
3.  (querer) probar los tacos al pastor.
4.  (salir) a caminar por el barrio de Coyoacán.

**Exercise 2**
A fortune teller is making predictions about your life next year. Fill in the blanks with the correct future simple conjugation of the verb provided.

1.  Tú y tus amigos ________ (hacer) un viaje inolvidable.
2.  Un nuevo proyecto profesional ________ (venir) a tu vida.
3.  Tus padres te ________ (decir) una noticia importante.
4.  Tú ________ (saber) la respuesta a una pregunta difícil.

**Exercise 3**
A planner is writing about their goals using the informal future (`ir a + infinitive`). Rewrite their statements using the more formal and definite future simple tense. Then, briefly explain the difference in nuance between the original and your new sentence.

Original sentence: "Mañana, voy a terminar el informe para mi jefe."

**Exercise 4**
Analyze the following sentence spoken by a Spanish learner. Identify the grammatical error in their use of the future tense, correct the sentence, and explain the rule they misunderstood.

Learner's sentence: "Para el proyecto, nosotros *estudiaramos* la historia y después *escribiremos* un ensayo."

**Exercise 5**
Complete the following short narrative by conjugating the verbs in parentheses. You will need to choose between the preterite, imperfect, and future tenses based on the context of the story.

El año pasado, mis hermanos y yo (1. decidir) que queríamos un cambio. La ciudad (2. ser) muy ruidosa y nosotros soñábamos con el campo. Por eso, el mes que viene, nos (3. mudar) a un pueblo pequeño. Yo ya sé lo que (4. hacer) primero: ¡adoptaré un perro!

**Exercise 6**
Your colleague is worried about an upcoming presentation. They say to you, "Estoy muy nervioso por la presentación de mañana. No sé si al cliente le gustará nuestra propuesta."

Write a two-sentence response in Spanish that uses the future simple to reassure them with a confident prediction and offer help.

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
1.  **visitaré** el Palacio de Bellas Artes.
2.  **podré** ver las pirámides de Teotihuacán.
3.  **querré** probar los tacos al pastor.
4.  **saldré** a caminar por el barrio de Coyoacán.

*Reasoning:* This exercise tests the direct application of future tense conjugation rules for the "yo" form.
- `visitar` is a regular -ar verb, so the standard `-é` ending is added to the infinitive: `visitaré`.
- `poder`, `querer`, and `salir` are irregular verbs. Their stems change to `podr-`, `querr-`, and `saldr-` respectively, before the standard `-é` ending is added.

**Answer 2**
1.  Tú y tus amigos **harán** (hacer) un viaje inolvidable.
2.  Un nuevo proyecto profesional **vendrá** (venir) a tu vida.
3.  Tus padres te **dirán** (decir) una noticia importante.
4.  Tú **sabrás** (saber) la respuesta a una pregunta difícil.

*Reasoning:* This exercise requires conjugating for different subjects within a sentence context.
- `Tú y tus amigos` is equivalent to `ustedes` (or `vosotros`), so the correct ending is `-án`. The verb `hacer` has an irregular stem: `har-`.
- `Un nuevo proyecto` is a third-person singular subject (`él/ella`), so the ending is `-á`. The verb `venir` has an irregular stem: `vendr-`.
- `Tus padres` is a third-person plural subject (`ellos`), so the ending is `-án`. The verb `decir` has an irregular stem: `dir-`.
- `Tú` is the second-person singular subject, so the ending is `-ás`. The verb `saber` has an irregular stem: `sabr-`.

**Answer 3**
New sentence: "Mañana, **terminaré** el informe para mi jefe."

*Reasoning:*
- **Transformation:** The future simple is formed by adding the appropriate ending (`-é` for "yo") directly to the infinitive (`terminar`).
- **Nuance:** The original sentence, "voy a terminar," uses the informal future. It expresses an intention or a plan that is about to happen. The new sentence, "terminaré," uses the future simple. This tense conveys a greater sense of certainty, formality, or a prediction about a future event. It's less about the immediate plan and more about the definite future outcome.

**Answer 4**
- **Error:** The word `estudiaramos` is incorrect.
- **Correction:** "Para el proyecto, nosotros **estudiaremos** la historia y después escribiremos un ensayo."
- **Explanation:** The learner incorrectly removed the `-ar` ending from `estudiar` before adding a future-like ending. The rule for regular verbs in the future simple is to keep the entire infinitive (`estudiar`) and add the ending for the subject (`-emos` for `nosotros`). The second verb, `escribiremos`, is already correct.

**Answer 5**
1.  **decidimos** (preterite)
2.  **era** (imperfect)
3.  **mudaremos** (future)
4.  **haré** (future)

*Reasoning:* This exercise requires synthesizing the future tense with past tenses.
1.  `decidimos`: This is a completed action in the past that moves the story forward. The preterite is appropriate.
2.  `era`: This describes the ongoing state or condition of the city in the past (it was noisy), setting the scene. The imperfect is used for descriptions and background information in the past.
3.  `mudaremos`: The phrase "el mes que viene" clearly indicates a future action. The subject is "nosotros," so the verb is `mudaremos`.
4.  `haré`: The speaker is stating what they will do in the future after moving. The subject is "yo," so the verb is `haré`.

**Answer 6**
A good response would be something like: "No te preocupes. Todo **saldrá** bien y al cliente le **encantará** la propuesta. Si quieres, yo te **ayudaré** a repasarla una última vez."

*Reasoning:* This answer demonstrates synthesis by using the future tense in a realistic, communicative context.
- `saldrá` and `encantará`: These are confident predictions used to reassure the colleague. The future simple is the correct tense for making such predictions about the outcome of the presentation.
- `ayudaré`: This is a promise or offer of a future action to provide concrete help.
The response directly addresses the colleague's anxiety using the target grammatical structure, showing mastery of its usage, not just its form.
