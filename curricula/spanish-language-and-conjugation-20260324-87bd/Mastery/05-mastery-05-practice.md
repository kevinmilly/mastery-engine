## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Analyze the following sentence: *Es una lástima que ellos no puedan venir a la fiesta.* Explain which specific expression triggers the use of the subjunctive verb *puedan*, and identify which category of the WEIRDO acronym this expression belongs to.

**Exercise 2**
You want your roommate to clean the kitchen. Formulate a direct request as a complete Spanish sentence using the verb *sugerir* (to suggest) and the verb *limpiar* (to clean). Ensure you use the correct mood and conjugation for the verb *limpiar*.

**Exercise 3**
Consider the following two statements. Explain the subtle but important difference in the speaker's certainty or perception of reality that is communicated by the choice between the indicative mood in sentence A and the subjunctive mood in sentence B.

A. *Busco al profesor que **habla** cinco idiomas.* (Indicative)
B. *Busco un profesor que **hable** cinco idiomas.* (Subjunctive)

**Exercise 4**
Read the short dialogue below. B's response contains a common grammatical error related to mood selection. Identify the incorrect verb, provide the corrected version, and explain the rule that makes the correction necessary.

A: *¿Crees que el nuevo restaurante es bueno?*
B: *No estoy seguro. Dudo que la comida **es** muy auténtica.*

**Exercise 5**
Imagine this scenario: Yesterday, your boss gave you a very important project (un proyecto muy importante) with a deadline for next week. Today, you are telling a colleague that you are worried you won't have enough time.

Construct a single, complex Spanish sentence that describes the past event (the boss giving you the project) and your present emotion (being worried). Your sentence must correctly use the preterite tense and the present subjunctive.

**Exercise 6**
Your friend is planning a trip to Peru but is worried about the high altitude in Cusco. Write a short paragraph (2-3 sentences) of advice. Your response must use at least two different types of WEIRDO triggers to introduce the subjunctive mood (e.g., a recommendation and an impersonal expression).

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The expression that triggers the subjunctive is *"Es una lástima que..."* (It's a shame that...).

This expression belongs to the **E (Emotion)** category of the WEIRDO acronym. The speaker is expressing an emotional reaction (pity, shame) to the fact that "they can't come to the party." The event is framed through the lens of the speaker's feelings, which requires the subjunctive mood for the verb *puedan*.

**Answer 2**
A correct sentence would be: *Te sugiero que limpies la cocina.*

**Reasoning:**
- The main clause verb *sugiero* (I suggest) is a verb of **R (Recommendation)** from the WEIRDO acronym.
- Verbs of recommendation in the main clause trigger the subjunctive mood in the subordinate clause.
- The subject of the main clause (yo) is different from the subject of the subordinate clause (tú).
- Therefore, the verb *limpiar* must be conjugated in the present subjunctive for the *tú* form, which is *limpies*.

**Answer 3**
The choice of mood reflects the existence of the professor.

- **Sentence A (Indicative):** *Busco al profesor que **habla** cinco idiomas.* The use of the indicative mood (*habla*) and the personal "a" implies that the speaker knows this specific professor exists and is looking for him. The professor's ability to speak five languages is presented as a known fact.
- **Sentence B (Subjunctive):** *Busco un profesor que **hable** cinco idiomas.* The use of the subjunctive mood (*hable*) implies that the speaker is looking for any professor who meets this criterion, but they do not know if such a person actually exists. The subjunctive is used for an antecedent that is uncertain, hypothetical, or nonexistent.

**Answer 4**
The incorrect verb is **es**. The corrected sentence is: *No estoy seguro. Dudo que la comida **sea** muy auténtica.*

**Reasoning:**
The main clause contains the verb *dudo que* (I doubt that). This expression belongs to the **D (Doubt/Denial)** category of the WEIRDO acronym. Expressions of doubt, disbelief, or denial trigger the use of the subjunctive in the clause that follows. The indicative form *es* incorrectly presents the food's authenticity as a certainty, which contradicts the doubt expressed in the main clause. The correct form is the present subjunctive *sea*.

**Answer 5**
A correct sentence would be: *Ayer mi jefe me dio un proyecto muy importante, y me preocupa que yo no **tenga** suficiente tiempo.* (Yesterday my boss gave me a very important project, and it worries me that I might not have enough time.)

**Reasoning:**
- **Preterite Tense:** The action of the boss giving the project (*dio*) is a completed event in the past, so the preterite is the correct choice. This demonstrates integration of prior knowledge of past tenses.
- **Present Subjunctive:** The second clause is triggered by the expression *"me preocupa que..."* (it worries me that...), which falls under the **E (Emotion)** category of WEIRDO. The speaker's worry about a present/future situation (*no tener tiempo*) requires the following verb to be in the present subjunctive, *tenga*.

**Answer 6**
An example response could be:
*Te recomiendo que hables con tu médico antes de viajar. También, es importante que bebas mucha agua y que descanses el primer día en Cusco para aclimatarte.*

**Reasoning:**
This response correctly synthesizes two different WEIRDO triggers:
1.  **Recommendation (R):** *"Te recomiendo que..."* triggers the subjunctive verb *hables* (from hablar).
2.  **Impersonal Expression (I):** *"Es importante que..."* triggers the subjunctive verbs *bebas* (from beber) and *descanses* (from descansar).

The paragraph provides realistic advice while correctly applying the subjunctive mood in multiple instances, demonstrating a high level of synthesis and application.
