## Capstone: Mastery — spanish language and conjugation

### The Scenario
You are a university student who has just returned from a transformative semester studying abroad in Granada, Spain. The experience was incredible, from navigating the winding streets of the Albaicín neighborhood to being mesmerized by the history of the Alhambra.

The university's Study Abroad office has asked you to write a feature post for their official blog. The goal is to share an authentic account of your experience to inspire and inform prospective students. They want a post that is both a personal reflection and a practical guide. Your blog post will serve as a key resource for students considering the Granada program for the following year. Your task is to write this post, following a specific four-part structure they have provided.

### Your Tasks
Your final deliverable is a single, cohesive blog post written entirely in Spanish. Structure your post with four distinct sections that correspond to the tasks below.

1.  **Narrate a Memorable Weekend:** Recount a specific weekend trip you took to a nearby city or town (e.g., Sevilla, Córdoba, or a small town in the Alpujarras). Tell the story of your trip. Describe what the place was like, what you and your friends were doing, and narrate the key events that happened from beginning to end.
2.  **Give Your Top Recommendations:** Based on your semester-long experience, offer three key pieces of advice for incoming students. What do you recommend they *do* to make the most of their time? What is essential that they *understand* before they go? Frame these as direct recommendations.
3.  **Explain Your Purpose & Acknowledge a Mentor:** Explain *why* you chose the Granada program in the first place (your purpose). Then, describe the single most helpful person you met there—a professor, a host family member, or a friend—*who* helped you acclimate.
4.  **Look to the Future:** Reflect on how this experience has changed you. First, state two concrete things you *will do* in the next year to continue your language journey. Second, describe what you *would do* differently if you had the opportunity to live in Spain for a full year instead of just one semester.

### What Good Work Looks Like
*   **Nuanced Past Narration:** Your response demonstrates a clear command of past tenses, skillfully using the preterite to narrate the main, completed actions of your story and the imperfect to provide background description, set the scene, and describe ongoing situations.
*   **Effective Use of Mood:** You correctly employ the subjunctive mood to give advice and express recommendations, showing you understand its use for subjective and non-factual statements. You also distinguish clearly between definite future plans (future tense) and hypothetical possibilities (conditional tense).
*   **Precision in Word Choice:** Your writing shows precision in its use of prepositions, especially in the clear and correct distinction between `por` (indicating cause, duration, or movement through) and `para` (indicating purpose, destination, or a recipient).
*   **Sophisticated Sentence Structure:** You construct complex and fluid sentences by effectively using relative pronouns (`que`, `quien`, etc.) to connect clauses and add descriptive detail, avoiding simple, choppy phrasing.
