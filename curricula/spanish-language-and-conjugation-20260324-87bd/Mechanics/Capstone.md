## Capstone: Mechanics — spanish language and conjugation

### The Scenario
You and your good friend from Madrid, Javier, are planning a surprise birthday party for another friend, Sofia. The party will be at your apartment this coming Saturday. Sofia thinks she is just coming over for a quiet movie night, so the element of surprise is crucial.

You and Javier have already invited all the guests. Now, you need to coordinate the final, crucial details for the party itself: the food, the decorations, and the music. Since Javier is busy with work, you are communicating via a series of short emails and text messages to make sure everything is ready for Saturday evening. Your goal is to clearly and efficiently coordinate the remaining tasks so the party is a success.

### Your Tasks
1.  **The Master Plan Email:** Write an email to Javier to get the planning started. In the email, state that you both have a lot to do (`tener que...`). Then, list three specific things you both need to prepare for the party (e.g., buy the food, choose the music, decorate the apartment). Use a different regular verb (-AR, -ER, and -IR) for each item on your list.

2.  **Delegating the Tasks:** Javier replies, "¡Perfecto! ¿Qué quieres que yo haga?" ("Perfect! What do you want me to do?"). Write a short response giving him two specific instructions. Use the affirmative tú command for both. One command must use a regular verb, and the other must use a stem-changing verb (o->ue), like *encontrar* or *recordar*.

3.  **The Pronoun Follow-up:** Javier agrees to your requests and asks a follow-up question about the birthday gift: "Compro el regalo para Sofía. ¿Cuándo se lo damos?" ("I'll buy the gift for Sofia. When do we give it to her?"). You want to handle the gift yourself to make sure it stays hidden. Respond to Javier by telling him, "**Give it to me** when you arrive at the apartment." Your sentence must use both a direct object pronoun (for "the gift") and an indirect object pronoun (for "me").

4.  **Day-Of Status Update:** It’s Saturday afternoon, just a few hours before the party. Javier sends you a text: "¿Cómo va todo?" ("How's everything going?"). Send him a text message back describing two different activities that are happening *right now*. One action should be something you are doing, and the other should be something another friend (e.g., "Ana") is doing. Use the present progressive tense (`estar` + gerund) for both actions.

### What Good Work Looks Like
*   **Demonstrates correct verb formation:** A strong response will accurately conjugate regular, stem-changing, and irregular verbs (like *tener* and *estar*) according to the subject and context of each task.
*   **Shows command of sentence structure:** Good work will correctly place pronouns, construct commands, and build sentences that are grammatically sound and logical, such as knowing when to use an infinitive versus a conjugated verb.
*   **Integrates concepts to create meaning:** The best responses will use different grammatical structures (like commands, present progressive, and object pronouns) purposefully to communicate distinct ideas clearly and efficiently, as one would in a real conversation.
*   **Exhibits precision in word choice:** A high-quality submission correctly applies the appropriate pronouns (direct vs. indirect) and prepositions to convey precise relationships between people, objects, and actions.
