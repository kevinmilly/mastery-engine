## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Your friend sends you a text message asking, "¿Qué haces?" (What are you doing?). You are currently listening to music. Using the verb *escuchar* and the subject *yo*, how would you reply in Spanish to say, "I am listening to music"?

**Exercise 2**
You are on a video call with your family. Your grandparents ask what your brothers, Pablo and Mateo, are doing. You see them in the kitchen preparing dinner. Using the verb *preparar* and the appropriate subject for "they," how would you describe the action in progress?

**Exercise 3**
A classmate is confused about when to use the simple present versus the present progressive. Explain the difference in meaning between these two sentences. Which one describes a routine, and which one describes an action happening at this very moment?

A) `Ana lee un libro de ciencia ficción.` (Ana reads a science fiction book.)
B) `Silencio, por favor. Ana está leyendo.` (Quiet, please. Ana is reading.)

**Exercise 4**
A beginner Spanish student wants to say, "My parents are sleeping." They know that the verb *dormir* (to sleep) is an *o->ue* stem-changing verb in the present tense (e.g., *yo duermo*). They write the sentence: `Mis padres están duermiendo.` This is incorrect. What is the correct gerund for *dormir*, and why is the student's logic incorrect for forming the present progressive?

**Exercise 5**
You are watching a very interesting movie (`una película muy interesante`). Your roommate walks in and asks what you are doing. You want to answer, "I am watching it," using a direct object pronoun to replace "the movie." Provide the two correct ways to form this sentence in Spanish, and explain the rule for pronoun placement in each structure.

**Exercise 6**
Your friend is asking you for advice. You want to reply, "I am telling you the truth" (`la verdad`). The verb "to tell" is *decir*, which has an *e->i* stem change in the gerund. Combine this knowledge with the correct indirect object pronoun for "you" (*te*). Construct both possible correct sentences in Spanish.

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The correct reply is: `(Yo) estoy escuchando música.`

**Reasoning:**
The present progressive is formed with the present tense of *estar* + the gerund of the main verb.
1.  The *yo* form of *estar* is `estoy`.
2.  The verb is *escuchar*, an -AR verb. To form the gerund, you drop the -AR and add -ando. `escuchar` -> `escuchando`.
3.  Combining these parts gives you `estoy escuchando`.

**Answer 2**
The correct sentence is: `(Ellos) están preparando la cena.`

**Reasoning:**
1.  The subject is "they" (Pablo and Mateo), which is `ellos` in Spanish. The `ellos` form of *estar* is `están`.
2.  The verb is *preparar*, an -AR verb. The gerund is formed by dropping the -AR and adding -ando. `preparar` -> `preparando`.
3.  The complete structure is `están preparando`.

**Answer 3**
Sentence A describes a routine or a general fact, while Sentence B describes an action currently in progress.

**Reasoning:**
*   **A) `Ana lee un libro de ciencia ficción.`** This is the simple present tense. It implies that Ana reads this type of book in general, or perhaps that she is in the process of reading one over a long period, but not necessarily at this exact second. It describes a habit or state.
*   **B) `Silencio, por favor. Ana está leyendo.`** This is the present progressive tense. The use of `estar + gerund` specifically emphasizes that the action of reading is happening *right now*, at the moment of speaking, which is why the speaker is asking for quiet.

**Answer 4**
The correct sentence is: `Mis padres están durmiendo.`

**Reasoning:**
The student's logic is flawed because the stem-change rule for the present tense does not always apply to the gerund.
1.  **Present Tense Stem Change:** In the present tense, the verb *dormir* changes from *o -> ue* (e.g., `duermo`, `duermes`, `duerme`).
2.  **Gerund Stem Change:** For -IR verbs that stem-change in the present tense, the gerund has a different, simplified stem change. The *o -> ue* change becomes *o -> u*. Therefore, the gerund of *dormir* is `durmiendo`.
The student incorrectly applied the present tense `ue` stem change to the gerund form.

**Answer 5**
The two correct sentences are:
1. `La estoy viendo.`
2. `Estoy viéndola.`

**Reasoning:**
The direct object "the movie" (`la película`) is feminine, so its pronoun is `la`. When using a pronoun with the present progressive, there are two valid placements:
1.  **Before the conjugated verb:** The pronoun `la` can be placed before the entire verb phrase (`estoy viendo`).
2.  **Attached to the gerund:** The pronoun `la` can be attached directly to the end of the gerund (`viendo`). When you attach a pronoun to a gerund that has two or more syllables, you must add a written accent to the vowel that was originally stressed to maintain the correct pronunciation (`vi**e**ndo` -> `vi**é**ndola`).

**Answer 6**
The two correct sentences are:
1. `Te estoy diciendo la verdad.`
2. `Estoy diciéndote la verdad.`

**Reasoning:**
This exercise combines knowledge of pronoun placement with the rule for stem-changing gerunds.
1.  **Gerund Formation:** The verb is *decir*. It is an -IR verb with an *e->i* stem change in the gerund. So, `decir` -> `diciendo`.
2.  **Pronoun:** The indirect object "you" (informal) is `te`.
3.  **Placement Rules:** Just like with direct object pronouns (Exercise 5), the indirect object pronoun `te` can either go before the conjugated verb *estar* (`Te estoy diciendo...`) or be attached to the end of the gerund (`...diciéndote`). An accent is added to `diciendo` to preserve its original stress.
