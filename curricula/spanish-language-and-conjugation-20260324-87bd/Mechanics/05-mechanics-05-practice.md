## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Complete the sentence with the correct form of the verb 'tener'.

*Tú y tus hermanos ___________ mucha suerte de viajar a México este verano.* (You and your siblings are very lucky to travel to Mexico this summer.)

**Exercise 2**
A child runs to their mother after seeing a spider on the wall. The child is shaking and pointing at the spider. Which 'tener' expression best describes the child's state?

**Exercise 3**
In English, we say "I am right" to express that our statement is correct. How would you express this same concept in Spanish using a 'tener' expression? Create a complete sentence as if you are the one speaking.

**Exercise 4**
Your friend calls you at 11:00 PM on a Tuesday. You have to wake up for work at 6:00 AM the next morning. Using a 'tener' expression, write a Spanish sentence explaining why you need to end the phone call.

**Exercise 5**
Complete the short paragraph below by conjugating the verbs in parentheses. One is 'tener', and the others are regular verbs.

*Mi familia y yo ________ (vivir) en una casa pequeña. Nosotros no ________ (tener) un perro, pero ________ (aprender) mucho sobre los animales en la escuela.*

**Exercise 6**
Your friend Marta is trying to decide what to do this evening. Fill in the blanks with the correct present tense forms of 'tener' or 'querer' (a stem-changing e->ie verb). Pay close attention to the context of each sentence.

*Marta ________ (querer) ver una película, pero ella ________ (tener) que estudiar. Sus amigos ________ (tener) dos entradas para un concierto, pero ella no ________ (querer) ir porque está muy cansada.*

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
**Answer:** *tienen*

**Reasoning:** The subject of the sentence is "Tú y tus hermanos" (You and your siblings). In Spain, this would correspond to the *vosotros* form. However, in Latin America (and most standard Spanish courses), this group is referred to with the third-person plural pronoun *ustedes*. The correct third-person plural conjugation of 'tener' is *tienen*.

**Answer 2**
**Answer:** *Tener miedo* (to be afraid/scared).

**Reasoning:** The child's actions (shaking, pointing) are classic signs of fear. The other common 'tener' expressions do not fit the context: *tener hambre* (to be hungry), *tener sed* (to be thirsty), *tener frío* (to be cold), or *tener prisa* (to be in a hurry).

**Answer 3**
**Answer:** *Yo tengo razón.*

**Reasoning:** The English idiom "I am right" does not translate literally using the verb *ser* or *estar*. The correct and natural Spanish expression is *tener razón* (literally, "to have reason"). The question asks for a complete sentence from your perspective, so you must use the first-person singular form, *tengo*.

**Answer 4**
**Answer:** A correct sentence would be: *Tengo sueño.* (I am sleepy.) or *Tengo que levantarme temprano.* (I have to get up early.)

**Reasoning:** The context is that it is late and you need to wake up early. The most direct feeling is sleepiness, expressed with *tener sueño*. Alternatively, you can express the obligation using the structure *tener que + infinitive*, which means "to have to do something." Both answers correctly apply a 'tener' expression to the realistic scenario.

**Answer 5**
**Answer:** *vivimos, tenemos, aprendemos*

**Reasoning:**
1.  *vivimos*: The subject is "Mi familia y yo," which is equivalent to *nosotros* (we). *Vivir* is a regular -IR verb, and the *nosotros* form is *vivimos*.
2.  *tenemos*: The subject is still *nosotros*. *Tener* is irregular, but its *nosotros* form, *tenemos*, does not have a stem change.
3.  *aprendemos*: The subject is still *nosotros*. *Aprender* is a regular -ER verb, and the *nosotros* form is *aprendemos*. This exercise requires applying the correct regular and irregular conjugations for the same subject.

**Answer 6**
**Answer:** *quiere, tiene, tienen, quiere*

**Reasoning:** This exercise requires distinguishing between the conjugation patterns of 'tener' and the stem-changing verb 'querer' (e->ie).
1.  *quiere*: The subject is Marta (*ella*). *Querer* is a stem-changing verb, so the 'e' changes to 'ie' in the third-person singular: *quiere*.
2.  *tiene*: The subject is still Marta (*ella*). *Tener* is also a stem-changing verb (e->ie) in this form, so it becomes *tiene*.
3.  *tienen*: The subject changes to "Sus amigos" (*ellos*). The third-person plural form of 'tener' is *tienen*, which also has the e->ie stem change.
4.  *quiere*: The subject returns to Marta (*ella*). As in the first blank, the correct form is *quiere*.
