## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
A friend is telling you about their family's weekend routine. They say, "On Saturdays, my parents..." and want to add the verb *descansar* (to rest). What is the correct present tense form of the verb to complete this sentence?

**Exercise 2**
You are texting a group of friends to make plans. You want to ask, "Do you all dance at the party?" using the verb *bailar* (to dance) and assuming you are in Spain (using the *vosotros/as* form). How would you write this question?

**Exercise 3**
You read the following sentence in a book: "El perro camina por el parque." Identify the infinitive form of the verb and explain why the verb ends in "-a" in this sentence.

**Exercise 4**
A fellow learner writes the sentence: "Yo escucho a la música." You notice they used the correct verb form for *yo*, but your friend who is a native speaker says a more common verb for "to listen to" is *escuchar*. The learner then tries to correct their sentence for a group of people: "Nosotros escuchamos a la música." Is this second sentence grammatically correct? Why or why not?

(Focus only on the verb conjugation.)

**Exercise 5**
You are trying to describe what is happening in a busy café. Fill in the blanks with the correct present tense conjugations of the verbs in parentheses.

"Un hombre (usar) la computadora. Dos mujeres (conversar) y (tomar) café. Yo (necesitar) estudiar, pero (mirar) a la gente."

**Exercise 6**
You are writing a formal email to your new boss, Sra. López, to ask what she needs. You know the verb for "to need" is *necesitar*. Which of the following questions is the most appropriate to use, and why are the other two incorrect for this specific situation?

A) ¿Qué necesitas?
B) ¿Qué necesita usted?
C) ¿Qué necesitan ellos?

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The correct form is **descansan**.

**Reasoning:** The subject of the verb is "my parents" (*mis padres*). This is a third-person plural subject, which corresponds to the pronoun *ellos* (they). For regular -AR verbs, the ending for *ellos* is "-an". You start with the infinitive *descansar*, remove the -AR ending to get the stem *descans-*, and add the correct ending: *descans* + *an* = *descansan*.

**Answer 2**
The correct question is **¿Bailáis en la fiesta?**

**Reasoning:** The subject is "you all" in Spain, which corresponds to the pronoun *vosotros*. To conjugate *bailar* for *vosotros*, you remove the -AR ending to get the stem *bail-* and add the *vosotros* ending, which is "-áis". The result is *bailáis*.

**Answer 3**
The infinitive is **caminar**. The verb ends in "-a" because the subject of the sentence, "el perro" (the dog), is a singular, third-person noun.

**Reasoning:** This subject corresponds to the pronoun *él* (he/it). The conjugation pattern for the *él/ella/usted* form of regular -AR verbs is to add the ending "-a" to the verb stem (*camin-*).

**Answer 4**
Yes, the sentence "Nosotros escuchamos a la música" is grammatically correct from a conjugation standpoint.

**Reasoning:** The subject is *nosotros* (we). For regular -AR verbs, the pattern for *nosotros* is to remove the -AR from the infinitive (*escuchar*) to find the stem (*escuch-*) and add the ending "-amos". Therefore, *escuchamos* is the correct conjugation for the subject *nosotros*. (Note: While the "a" is often omitted in "escuchar música," the verb form itself is correct.)

**Answer 5**
The completed sentences are:
"Un hombre **usa** la computadora. Dos mujeres **conversan** y **toman** café. Yo **necesito** estudiar, pero **miro** a la gente."

**Reasoning:**
- **usa:** The subject is "Un hombre" (a man), which is third-person singular (*él*). The ending is "-a".
- **conversan:** The subject is "Dos mujeres" (two women), which is third-person plural (*ellas*). The ending is "-an".
- **toman:** The subject is still "Dos mujeres," so the same plural ending "-an" applies.
- **necesito:** The subject is "Yo" (I). The ending is "-o".
- **miro:** The subject is still "Yo" (I). The ending is "-o".

**Answer 6**
The most appropriate question is **B) ¿Qué necesita usted?**

**Reasoning:**
- **Option B is correct** because you are addressing your boss, Sra. López, in a formal context. The formal "you" is *usted*, and the correct conjugation for *usted* with the verb *necesitar* is *necesita*.
- **Option A is incorrect** because *necesitas* is the conjugation for *tú*, the informal "you". Using this form would be overly familiar and potentially disrespectful to a new boss.
- **Option C is incorrect** because *necesitan* is the conjugation for *ellos/ellas/ustedes* (they/you all). This is the wrong form because you are speaking to only one person (Sra. López), not a group of people or about other people.
