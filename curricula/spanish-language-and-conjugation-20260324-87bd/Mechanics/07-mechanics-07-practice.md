## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Rewrite the following sentence, replacing the indirect object "a mis amigos" with the correct indirect object pronoun. The pronoun should be placed before the conjugated verb.

Original sentence: *Yo escribo una carta a mis amigos.* (I write a letter to my friends.)

**Exercise 2**
A mother is talking to her son, Tomás. She wants him to understand that she buys candy *for him*. Choose the correct indirect object pronoun to complete her sentence.

Sentence: *Tomás, yo ___ compro dulces porque te quiero.* (Tomás, I buy you candy because I love you.)
Options: me, te, le, les

**Exercise 3**
In Spanish, when you have a conjugated verb followed by an infinitive (like *querer comprar* - to want to buy), the indirect object pronoun can be placed in two different positions.

Rewrite the sentence "Ella quiere dar un regalo a nosotros" in two grammatically correct ways using an indirect object pronoun.

**Exercise 4**
Consider the two sentences below. One requires a direct object pronoun and one requires an indirect object pronoun to replace the phrase "a la profesora". Fill in each blank with the correct pronoun (*la* or *le*) and explain why you chose it.

1.  *Yo veo a la profesora en la clase.* -> *Yo ___ veo en la clase.*
2.  *Yo pregunto algo a la profesora.* -> *Yo ___ pregunto algo.*

**Exercise 5**
Imagine a friend asks you: *¿Por qué no me dices la verdad?* (Why don't you tell me the truth?). You want to respond that you don't tell the truth *to him/her* because you have a secret.

Using the stem-changing verb *tener* (yo tengo), construct a full sentence in Spanish that means: "I don't tell you the truth because I have a secret." Your answer must use an indirect object pronoun.

**Exercise 6**
The sentence *Le preparo la cena* is grammatically correct, but it's ambiguous. The pronoun *le* could refer to *él* (him), *ella* (her), or *usted* (you, formal).

A speaker wants to say this sentence but needs to be very clear about the recipient. How would the speaker say "I prepare dinner for him" and "I prepare dinner for you (formal)" to remove all ambiguity?

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
Correct sentence: *Yo les escribo una carta.*

**Reasoning:** The indirect object is "a mis amigos" (to my friends), which is the recipient of the action of writing. Since "mis amigos" is a third-person plural group, the correct indirect object pronoun is *les*. The pronoun is placed directly before the conjugated verb *escribo*.

**Answer 2**
Correct pronoun: *te*

**Reasoning:** The mother is speaking directly to Tomás (the "tú" form). The candy is *for him*. The indirect object pronoun that corresponds to "tú" is *te*. The sentence becomes: *Tomás, yo te compro dulces porque te quiero.*

**Answer 3**
The two correct sentences are:
1.  *Ella nos quiere dar un regalo.*
2.  *Ella quiere darnos un regalo.*

**Reasoning:** The indirect object is "a nosotros" (to us), so the pronoun is *nos*. In a conjugated verb + infinitive structure, the pronoun can either be placed before the conjugated verb (*quiere*) or attached to the end of the infinitive (*dar*). Both placements are correct.

**Answer 4**
1.  *Yo la veo en la clase.*
    **Reasoning:** In this sentence, "la profesora" is the *direct* object. She is the person being directly seen. The verb *ver* (to see) acts directly upon her. The feminine, singular direct object pronoun is *la*.
2.  *Yo le pregunto algo.*
    **Reasoning:** In this sentence, "la profesora" is the *indirect* object. She is the recipient of the question ("algo"). The thing being directly acted upon (asked) is "algo" (something). Therefore, we use the indirect object pronoun *le*.

**Answer 5**
Correct sentence: *No te digo la verdad porque tengo un secreto.*

**Reasoning:** The question was *¿Por qué no me dices la verdad?*, with the indirect object pronoun *me* (to me). The answer must be directed back at the speaker, so the pronoun must be *te* (to you). The verb *tener* is conjugated to *tengo* in the first-person singular ("I have"). The final sentence correctly combines the pronoun *te* with the verb *digo* (from *decir*) and the conjugated form of *tener*.

**Answer 6**
1.  For him: *Le preparo la cena a él.*
2.  For you (formal): *Le preparo la cena a usted.*

**Reasoning:** The pronoun *le* remains in the sentence, but to resolve the ambiguity, a clarifying prepositional phrase (*a* + pronoun/noun) is added at the end of the sentence. This is a common and necessary feature of Spanish to specify the recipient when using the pronouns *le* or *les*. Adding *a él* specifies the recipient is "him," and adding *a usted* specifies the recipient is "you (formal)."
