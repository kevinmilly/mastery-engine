## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
A friend sends you a text message asking what you and your family are doing. You want to reply that "we eat" and "he (your brother) reads." Based on the verbs `comer` (to eat) and `leer` (to read), provide the correct Spanish verb forms for "we eat" and "he reads."

**Exercise 2**
Complete the following sentences by conjugating the verb in parentheses into the correct present tense form. This demonstrates how verbs change to match the subject in a complete thought.

1.  Yo ______ (aprender) las reglas de conjugación. (I learn the conjugation rules.)
2.  Tú ______ (correr) en el parque todos los días. (You run in the park every day.)

**Exercise 3**
Your classmate has written the following sentence, but it contains an error in verb conjugation: "Los estudiantes `comprendo` la lección, pero la profesora `cree` que no."

Identify the incorrect verb, explain why it is wrong based on its subject, and provide the correct conjugation.

**Exercise 4**
You know that for the `nosotros` form, regular -AR verbs end in `-amos` (e.g., `hablamos`) and regular -ER verbs end in `-emos` (e.g., `comemos`).

Now, consider the `vosotros` (you all, informal) form. Based on the pattern shift you see between `-amos` and `-emos`, what is the logical ending for a regular -ER verb in the `vosotros` form, given that the -AR ending is `-áis` (e.g., `habláis`)? Explain your reasoning.

**Exercise 5**
Complete the short paragraph below, which requires you to use both regular -AR and -ER verb conjugations. This tests your ability to switch between the two patterns based on the verb you see.

"Mi hermana y yo tenemos rutinas diferentes. Por la mañana, yo ______ (beber) café y ______ (repasar) mis notas. Ella no ______ (beber) café; en su lugar, ella ______ (caminar) con su perro."
(My sister and I have different routines. In the morning, I ______ (to drink) coffee and ______ (to review) my notes. She doesn't ______ (to drink) coffee; instead, she ______ (to walk) with her dog.)

**Exercise 6**
Write two original sentences in Spanish that describe a typical weekly activity.
- The first sentence must use the verb `vender` (to sell) and describe what a specific person (`él` or `ella`) sells.
- The second sentence must use the verb `aprender` (to learn) and describe something you and your friends (`nosotros`) learn.

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
-   **we eat:** `comemos`
-   **he reads:** `lee`

**Reasoning:**
1.  For "we eat," the subject is `nosotros` (we). The verb is `comer`. To conjugate, you remove the `-er` to get the stem `com-` and add the `nosotros` ending for -ER verbs, which is `-emos`. `com-` + `-emos` = `comemos`.
2.  For "he reads," the subject is `él` (he). The verb is `leer`. You remove the `-er` to get the stem `le-` and add the `él` ending, which is `-e`. `le-` + `-e` = `lee`.

**Answer 2**
1.  Yo **aprendo** las reglas de conjugación.
2.  Tú **corres** en el parque todos los días.

**Reasoning:**
1.  The subject is `yo` (I). For the verb `aprender`, the stem is `aprend-`. The `yo` ending for regular -ER verbs is `-o`. Therefore, the correct form is `aprendo`.
2.  The subject is `tú` (you, informal). For the verb `correr`, the stem is `corr-`. The `tú` ending for regular -ER verbs is `-es`. Therefore, the correct form is `corres`.

**Answer 3**
-   **Incorrect verb:** `comprendo`
-   **Explanation:** The subject of the verb is "Los estudiantes" (the students), which corresponds to the `ellos` (they) pronoun. The conjugation `comprendo` is the `yo` (I) form. The verb must match its subject.
-   **Correct conjugation:** `comprenden`

**Reasoning:** The verb `comprender` must be conjugated for the subject `los estudiantes` (they). To do this, you take the stem `comprend-` and add the `ellos/ellas/ustedes` ending for -ER verbs, which is `-en`.

**Answer 4**
-   **Logical ending:** `-éis`
-   **Example verb:** `coméis`

**Reasoning:** The primary difference between -AR and -ER verb endings (after the `yo` form) is that the "a" vowel in the -AR ending is replaced by an "e" in the -ER ending.
-   `nosotros`: habl**a**mos -> com**e**mos
-   `vosotros`: The pattern follows. The -AR ending `-áis` becomes `-éis` for -ER verbs. So, for the verb `comer`, the `vosotros` form is `coméis`.

**Answer 5**
"Mi hermana y yo tenemos rutinas diferentes. Por la mañana, yo **bebo** café y **repaso** mis notas. Ella no **bebe** café; en su lugar, ella **camina** con su perro."

**Reasoning:**
-   `yo ______ (beber)`: Subject is `yo`, verb is -ER. Stem `beb-` + `-o` = `bebo`.
-   `y ______ (repasar)`: Subject is still `yo`, verb is -AR. Stem `repas-` + `-o` = `repaso`.
-   `Ella no ______ (beber)`: Subject is `ella`, verb is -ER. Stem `beb-` + `-e` = `bebe`.
-   `ella ______ (caminar)`: Subject is `ella`, verb is -AR. Stem `camin-` + `-a` = `camina`.
