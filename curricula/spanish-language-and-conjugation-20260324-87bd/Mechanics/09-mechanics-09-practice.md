## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Your friend is learning to cook and says, "Yo aprendo a cortar las verduras" (I am learning to cut the vegetables). To encourage them, you want to say, "Learn to cut the vegetables!" How would you form the affirmative tú command using the regular verb `aprender`?

**Exercise 2**
You are trying to fix a wobbly table with your friend, and they are rushing. You need to tell them, "Have patience!" Which irregular command form of the verb `tener` would you use to complete the sentence: "¡____ paciencia!"?

**Exercise 3**
You and your friend are talking about a movie. You suggest, "Tú pides las entradas en línea" (You ask for the tickets online). How would you change this suggestion into a direct command telling your friend to ask for the tickets? The verb `pedir` is a stem-changing verb (e->i).

**Exercise 4**
A beginner Spanish speaker tells their friend, "Hablas más despacio, por favor," intending to say, "Speak more slowly, please." Why is "hablas" incorrect as an affirmative tú command, and what is the correct form?

**Exercise 5**
Your roommate has left their textbook (`el libro`) on the kitchen table. You want to tell them, "Put it on the shelf!" How do you combine the command form of `poner` with the direct object pronoun `lo` to give this instruction? Explain any changes you need to make to the word.

**Exercise 6**
Your friend can't find their keys (`las llaves`), but you can see them. You want to say, "Look for them for me!" How would you form this command using the verb `buscar`, the indirect object pronoun `me`, and the direct object pronoun `las`? Be sure to explain the pronoun order and any necessary accent marks.

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The correct command is: **Aprende** a cortar las verduras.

**Reasoning:** The rule for forming regular affirmative tú commands is to use the third-person singular (él/ella/usted) form of the present tense. For the verb `aprender`, the third-person singular form is `aprende`.

**Answer 2**
The correct command is: ¡**Ten** paciencia!

**Reasoning:** `Tener` is one of the eight common verbs with irregular affirmative tú commands. While its third-person form is `tiene`, its irregular command form is `ten`.

**Answer 3**
The correct command is: **Pide** las entradas en línea.

**Reasoning:** The rule for forming the command (using the third-person singular form) still applies to stem-changing verbs. The verb `pedir` changes its stem from `e` to `i` in the present tense for most subjects, including the third-person singular. Thus, `él pide` becomes the command `pide`.

**Answer 4**
"Hablas" is incorrect because it is the second-person informal (`tú`) present indicative form, not the command form. The speaker is stating a fact ("you speak") instead of giving an order.

The correct command is **habla**.

**Reasoning:** To form the affirmative tú command for a regular verb like `hablar`, you must use the third-person singular (él/ella/usted) present tense form, which is `habla`.

**Answer 5**
The correct command is: ¡**Ponlo** en el estante!

**Reasoning:**
1.  **Form the command:** `Poner` is an irregular verb, and its affirmative tú command is `pon`.
2.  **Identify the pronoun:** The direct object pronoun for `el libro` (it) is `lo`.
3.  **Attach the pronoun:** In affirmative commands, pronouns are attached directly to the end of the verb: `ponlo`.
4.  **Check for accents:** The original command `pon` is a single-syllable word. Adding one pronoun does not shift the natural stress, so no accent mark is needed.

**Answer 6**
The correct command is: ¡**Búscamelas**!

**Reasoning:**
1.  **Form the command:** The command form of the regular verb `buscar` is `busca` (the él/ella/usted present form).
2.  **Order the pronouns:** The rule is that the indirect object pronoun (`me`) comes before the direct object pronoun (`las`).
3.  **Attach the pronouns:** Attach "me" and then "las" to the end of the command: `buscamelas`.
4.  **Add an accent mark:** The original stress in `busca` is on the first syllable: **bús**-ca. When two syllables (`-me-las`) are added, the new word is `bus-ca-me-las`. To preserve the original stress on the `u`, an accent mark is required: `búscamelas`.
