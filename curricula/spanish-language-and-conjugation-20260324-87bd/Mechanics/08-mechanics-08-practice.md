## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
Complete the following sentence with the correct preposition (*a, de, en*). Explain your choice.

"Mi hermano vive ___ Madrid."

**Exercise 2**
Choose the correct preposition (*con, para, por*) to complete the sentence and translate the final sentence into English.

"Compro este regalo ___ mi amiga."

**Exercise 3**
Consider the two sentences below. Both use the preposition 'de', but it translates differently into English.

1.  El coche **de** Juan es rojo. (Juan's car is red.)
2.  Yo soy **de** Argentina. (I am from Argentina.)

Explain the two different functions that the preposition 'de' is performing in these sentences.

**Exercise 4**
A friend learning Spanish writes the following incorrect sentence: "Camino *para* el parque para ver los árboles." The intended meaning is: "I walk through the park to see the trees."

Identify the incorrect preposition and explain why it is wrong. What preposition should be used instead?

**Exercise 5**
Using your knowledge of verb conjugation and indirect object pronouns, combine the following elements into a single, grammatically correct Spanish sentence. You will need to use the prepositions 'a' and 'con'.

Elements:
-   (yo)
-   *dar* (conjugated in the present tense)
-   un consejo (a piece of advice)
-   mi amigo
-   paciencia

**Exercise 6**
You want to tell your friend that you are traveling to Mexico for two weeks with your family. Translate this idea into a complete Spanish sentence. Your sentence must correctly use the prepositions *a, por,* and *con*, and the verb *viajar* must be conjugated correctly.

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The correct sentence is: "Mi hermano vive **en** Madrid."

**Reasoning:** The preposition 'en' is used to indicate location, meaning "in," "on," or "at." Since Madrid is the location where the brother lives, 'en' is the appropriate choice. 'A' is used for movement towards a destination ("Voy a Madrid"), and 'de' is used for origin ("Soy de Madrid").

**Answer 2**
The correct preposition is 'para'. The sentence is: "Compro este regalo **para** mi amiga."

**Translation:** "I am buying this gift for my friend."

**Reasoning:** 'Para' is used to indicate the recipient or destination of an object or action. In this case, the friend is the recipient of the gift. 'Por' would imply an exchange (e.g., "in exchange for my friend"), and 'con' would mean "with," which doesn't fit the context.

**Answer 3**
**Reasoning:**
1.  In "El coche **de** Juan," 'de' is used to show possession or relationship. It functions like an apostrophe-s ('s) in English, indicating that the car belongs to Juan.
2.  In "Yo soy **de** Argentina," 'de' is used to indicate origin. It specifies the place a person or thing comes from, translating to "from" in English.

This demonstrates that a single Spanish preposition can cover multiple concepts that require different words in English.

**Answer 4**
The incorrect preposition is the first "para". The correct preposition should be "por".

**Reasoning:**
-   **'Para'** indicates a destination or purpose (the *goal*). The purpose of walking is "para ver los árboles" (to see the trees), so the second 'para' is correct.
-   **'Por'** is used to indicate movement *through* or *along* a place. The person is walking *through* the park, not *to* the park as their final destination in this context.

The corrected sentence is: "Camino **por** el parque para ver los árboles."

**Answer 5**
The correct sentence is: "**Le** doy un consejo **a** mi amigo **con** paciencia."

**Reasoning:**
1.  The verb *dar* is conjugated for (yo): *doy*.
2.  The indirect object is "mi amigo". The preposition 'a' is required before an indirect object that is a person ("a mi amigo").
3.  The indirect object pronoun 'le' (for 'him') is required and is placed before the conjugated verb.
4.  The preposition 'con' is used to mean "with," indicating the manner in which the advice is given ("con paciencia" - with patience).

**Answer 6**
A correct translation is: "(Yo) viajo **a** México **por** dos semanas **con** mi familia."

**Reasoning:**
-   **Viajo:** The verb *viajar* is correctly conjugated in the first-person present tense.
-   **a México:** The preposition 'a' is used to indicate movement toward a destination.
-   **por dos semanas:** The preposition 'por' is used to express a duration of time ("for two weeks"). 'Para' would not be correct here as it indicates a deadline, not duration.
-   **con mi familia:** The preposition 'con' is used to mean "with," indicating accompaniment.
