## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
You need to replace the batteries in a remote control, but the battery compartment is held closed by a small screw with a single, straight slot in its head. Which specific tool should you select, and why would using a screwdriver with a cross-shaped tip be a bad choice for this task?

**Exercise 2**
You are hanging a series of four picture frames, and you want the top edge of each frame to be exactly 68 and 5/8 inches from the floor. Describe precisely where on a standard tape measure (marked in 1/16th inch increments) you would find this measurement.

**Exercise 3**
You are assembling a flat-pack bookshelf. The instructions require you to tighten several hex bolts to attach the shelves. You grab an adjustable wrench and fit it onto the first bolt. As you pull, the wrench slips off and rounds the corners of the bolt head. What is the most likely user error that caused this, and what is the corrective action?

**Exercise 4**
A friend is trying to drive a large nail into a dense piece of lumber. They are holding the hammer loosely at the middle of the handle and taking short, quick swings. The nail keeps bending, and they are getting tired. Identify two specific changes they should make to their technique to drive the nail more effectively and safely.

**Exercise 5**
You need to remove an old, loose doorknob assembly that is attached with two long screws. From a previous lesson, you know you should wear safety glasses. Describe a potential hazard in this task that makes wearing gloves a good idea. Also, explain why you might need both a Phillips and a flathead screwdriver for this job.

**Exercise 6**
You notice a slight wobble in your dining room table leg. Upon inspection, you see the leg is attached to the tabletop frame by a large bolt that has become loose. The bolt is in a tight corner, right next to another table leg. Describe how you would choose and safely use a wrench for this repair, considering the limited space for movement. Integrate at least one key safety principle from the lesson.

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
**Tool:** A flathead screwdriver.

**Reasoning:** The screw is described as having a single, straight slot, which is the defining characteristic of a flathead screw. A flathead screwdriver tip is designed to fit this slot precisely. Using a Phillips (cross-shaped) screwdriver would be a bad choice because the tip would not sit securely in the straight slot. It would likely slip out, failing to turn the screw and potentially damaging the screw head (stripping it), making it much harder to remove later.

**Answer 2**
**Location:** You would find the 68-inch mark first. Then, you would count the smaller tick marks past it. The 5/8-inch mark is the 10th tick mark after the 68-inch line, as each 1/16 inch is one tick, and 5/8 is equal to 10/16. It is usually a medium-length line, halfway between the 1/2-inch (or 8/16) mark and the 3/4-inch (or 12/16) mark.

**Answer 3**
**User Error:** The most likely error is failing to tighten the adjustable jaw of the wrench snugly against the flats of the bolt head before applying force.

**Corrective Action:** The user should place the wrench on the bolt and turn the screw mechanism until the jaws are in firm, complete contact with the bolt head, with no wiggle room. This ensures the force is applied to the strong, flat sides of the bolt, not the weaker corners, preventing slippage and rounding.

**Answer 4**
**Technique Changes:**
1.  **Grip:** They should move their grip to the end of the hammer handle, away from the head. This maximizes leverage and allows the weight of the hammer head to do most of the work, reducing fatigue.
2.  **Swing:** They should use a longer, smoother swing that comes from the elbow and shoulder, not just the wrist. This generates more power and momentum, driving the nail in straight and preventing it from bending.

**Answer 5**
**Hazard for Gloves:** Older doorknobs, especially interior ones, can be made of plated metal that may have flaked or developed sharp burrs over time. When gripping the knob firmly to provide counter-pressure while unscrewing, these sharp edges could cut your hand. Gloves would protect against this.

**Reasoning for Two Screwdrivers:** Doorknob assemblies are often complex. While the main retaining screws might be Phillips head, the smaller set screw that holds the knob to the spindle is often a flathead screw. Having both tools on hand prevents being stopped midway through the job.

**Answer 6**
**Tool Choice and Use:** You would choose an adjustable wrench because the bolt size is unknown.

**Safe & Effective Technique:**
1.  **Adjust:** First, you would adjust the wrench jaws to fit the bolt head snugly to prevent slipping and stripping the bolt.
2.  **Position:** Due to the limited space, you can't make a long, sweeping pull. You will likely have to use a "box-end" grip or reposition the wrench after each short turn.
3.  **Safety Principle Integration:** The key principle is to always **pull the wrench toward your body** rather than pushing it away. In this tight space, pushing the wrench could cause your knuckles to slam into the adjacent table leg if the bolt suddenly loosens. By positioning your body so you can pull, you maintain better control and move your hand away from, not into, an obstacle.
