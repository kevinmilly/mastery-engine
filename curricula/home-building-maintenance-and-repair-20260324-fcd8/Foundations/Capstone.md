## Capstone: Foundations — home/building maintenance and repair

### The Scenario
You've just noticed a troubling sign in your first-floor living room: a faint, yellowish, circular water stain on the ceiling. It’s about 6 inches in diameter and feels slightly damp and cool to the touch, but it is not actively dripping.

Directly above this room is the main bathroom on the second floor. This bathroom contains a toilet, a single-vanity sink, and a combination bathtub/shower. Based on a simple house plan you have, the toilet is located almost directly over the stained area. The tub and sink are several feet away, on the other side of the bathroom. The ceiling is standard drywall, and you suspect the joists run parallel to the wall where the tub is located. Your immediate goal is not to perform the full repair, but to create a clear and safe plan to investigate the problem's source and prepare for the next steps.

### Your Tasks
1.  **Immediate Safety Protocol:** Before touching any tools or attempting to investigate, what are the first three critical actions you must take? For each action, briefly explain its purpose and why it's a necessary first step.

2.  **Diagnostic Analysis:** Based on the scenario and the likely location of the plumbing fixtures from the house plan, identify two different, plausible sources for this leak. For each potential source, explain your reasoning, connecting the fixture to the specific location and appearance of the stain.

3.  **Investigation Plan:** Outline a step-by-step plan to safely access the ceiling cavity to find and confirm the source of the leak. Your plan should specify the primary hand tool or power tool you would choose to make an inspection hole in the drywall and justify why that tool is the appropriate choice for this specific task.

4.  **Preventive Inspection Proposal:** Thinking beyond this single incident, create a simple, three-point weekly inspection checklist for this bathroom. The goal of the checklist is to proactively identify potential water issues before they can cause damage.

### What Good Work Looks Like
*   Demonstrates a methodical, safety-conscious approach, prioritizing protective measures and utility control before any diagnostic or destructive work begins.
*   Connects observable symptoms (the stain's location and appearance) to plausible root causes using logical deduction and a clear understanding of how home systems (plumbing, structural) interact.
*   Provides clear, actionable steps for the investigation, justifying tool choices based on their specific function, safety, and the materials involved.
*   Shows the ability to shift from a reactive "fix-it" mindset to a proactive one by proposing a simple, effective preventive maintenance routine.
