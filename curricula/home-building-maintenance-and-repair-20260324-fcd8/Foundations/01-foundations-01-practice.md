## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
You are getting ready to repaint a small, well-ventilated bedroom. You'll be using a standard water-based latex paint and a roller. Based on the task, what is the most essential piece of PPE you should wear, and what specific injury does it prevent?

**Exercise 2**
Your task is to change a lightbulb in a ceiling fixture that is 10 feet high. You plan to use a stepladder. Besides the ladder itself, what is the most critical site safety step you should take before you even begin climbing?

**Exercise 3**
You're helping a friend clear out their grandfather's old workshop in a garage. The space is filled with old lumber, rusty tools, and several unlabeled cans and jars of liquids. It is also very dusty. Identify three distinct types of hazards you are likely to face and name one piece of PPE appropriate for each.

**Exercise 4**
A homeowner is trying to unplug a sump pump in their flooded basement. They are standing in an inch of water while reaching for the electrical outlet. They are not wearing any special footwear or gloves. Identify the primary, most immediate hazard in this situation and explain why their actions are extremely dangerous.

**Exercise 5**
You need to saw through a single piece of pressure-treated lumber for an outdoor project. You have the following PPE available: sunglasses, thin cotton gardening gloves, and a simple cloth face covering for dust. Is this set of equipment adequate for the job? Explain your reasoning, focusing on what makes your selected equipment appropriate or inappropriate.

**Exercise 6**
You are about to start a project to demolish a small, old tile countertop in a kitchen. The project will create a lot of sharp debris and fine dust. Before you pick up the first tool, you need to prepare the site. Describe two preparatory actions you would take to manage the site itself (not including the PPE you will wear) to ensure the work is done safely and to minimize hazards to the rest of the house.

---

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The most essential piece of PPE is safety glasses or goggles. While painting a wall with a roller may seem low-risk, paint can easily splash or flick into your eyes, causing irritation, chemical burns, or serious eye injury. Gloves are also a good idea to prevent skin irritation, but eye protection is the most critical.

**Answer 2**
The most critical site safety step is to ensure the stepladder is placed on a firm, level, and non-slip surface. An unstable ladder is a primary cause of falls. Before climbing, you must verify that all four feet of the ladder are securely planted and that the surface isn't wet or uneven, which could cause it to shift unexpectedly.

**Answer 3**
The three distinct hazards and corresponding PPE are:
1.  **Chemical Hazard:** The unlabeled cans and jars could contain anything from solvents to pesticides. To protect your hands from absorption or chemical burns, you should wear chemical-resistant gloves (like nitrile or rubber), not cloth gloves.
2.  **Physical/Puncture Hazard:** Rusty tools and splintered lumber create a high risk of cuts and punctures. Sturdy, leather or cut-resistant work gloves are needed to protect your hands.
3.  **Respiratory Hazard:** Decades of accumulated dust could contain mold spores, insect droppings, or other harmful particles. A respirator mask (like an N95) is required to prevent inhalation of these airborne contaminants.

**Answer 4**
The primary hazard is electrocution. Water is an excellent conductor of electricity. By standing in water while interacting with an electrical outlet, the homeowner is creating a direct path for electricity to travel from the outlet, through their body, to the ground. This can cause severe injury or death. The proper procedure would be to turn off the power to that outlet at the circuit breaker before attempting to unplug anything.

**Answer 5**
No, this set of equipment is not adequate.
-   **Sunglasses are not a substitute for safety glasses.** They are not impact-rated and will not protect from flying debris created by the saw. ANSI Z87.1-rated safety glasses are required.
-   **Thin cotton gloves offer no real protection.** They will not prevent splinters from the pressure-treated wood and can get caught in the saw blade, pulling your hand toward the danger. Sturdier work gloves would be better, but for power tool use, some professionals recommend no gloves at all to avoid the catching hazard.
-   **A cloth face covering is insufficient.** Pressure-treated lumber contains chemicals that can be harmful if inhaled. A respirator mask rated for dust (e.g., N95) is necessary to filter out the fine sawdust particles.

**Answer 6**
Two effective site preparation actions would be:
1.  **Isolate the work area:** Use plastic sheeting and painter's tape to seal off doorways and cover any air vents. This contains the fine dust created during demolition, preventing it from spreading throughout the house and becoming a respiratory hazard for others.
2.  **Protect the floor:** Lay down heavy-duty cardboard, old blankets, or a durable tarp on the floor below the countertop. This creates a non-slip work surface and, more importantly, protects the floor from damage from falling debris and makes cleanup of sharp tile fragments much safer and easier.
