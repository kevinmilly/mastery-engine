## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
A recipe for buttermilk pancakes requires 1 cup of buttermilk. You only have whole milk. To replicate buttermilk's key chemical functions in the batter, you decide to add another ingredient to the whole milk. What single ingredient should you add, and what is the primary leavening-related function it helps the milk replicate?

**Exercise 2**
You are making a simple breading for fried fish, which calls for dredging the fish in seasoned all-purpose flour before frying. You want to make a gluten-free version for a guest. A friend suggests swapping the all-purpose flour with an equal amount of almond flour. Based on the functional properties of these flours, what is the most likely negative outcome for the texture and appearance of the final fried fish?

**Exercise 3**
A recipe for a rich vegetable soup instructs you to "mount with butter" at the very end, meaning you whisk in 2 tablespoons of cold, unsalted butter off the heat. Your goal is to make a dairy-free version of this soup. You have high-quality olive oil and refined coconut oil on hand. Which of these is the better substitute for achieving the specific functions of "mounting," and what key difference in the final product should you expect compared to using butter?

**Exercise 4**
A mayonnaise recipe consists primarily of egg yolk, oil, and an acid (like lemon juice or vinegar). The egg yolk acts as the primary emulsifier. You need to make a vegan version. You decide to use an immersion blender and aquafaba (the liquid from a can of chickpeas) as the emulsifier. Given that aquafaba is mostly water and protein, what adjustment will you likely need to make to the ratio of oil to the other ingredients, and why is this adjustment necessary to form a stable emulsion?

**Exercise 5**
You are adapting a standard chocolate chip cookie recipe to have a softer, chewier texture and a more complex, less one-dimensionally sweet flavor. The original recipe calls for all-purpose flour, granulated white sugar, and unsalted butter. Propose one specific substitution for *each* of these three ingredients to achieve your stated goals. Justify each choice by explaining how it alters the cookie's chemistry and final texture/flavor profile.

**Exercise 6**
You are making a lemon curd, which gets its thick, rich texture from the coagulation of egg yolk proteins when heated with sugar and acid (lemon juice). A guest has a severe egg allergy. You plan to create an egg-free version using cornstarch as the primary thickener. Identify two distinct functional roles of the egg yolks in the original curd that the cornstarch *cannot* replicate. For each missing function, describe an additional ingredient or technique you would use to compensate.

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The best single ingredient to add is an acid, such as lemon juice or white vinegar (about 1 tablespoon per cup of milk). The primary function being replicated is providing acidity. In the original recipe, the acidity of the buttermilk reacts with an alkaline leavener (like baking soda) to produce carbon dioxide gas, which makes the pancakes light and fluffy. Adding an acid to the whole milk creates a chemically similar environment, allowing for the same leavening reaction to occur.

**Answer 2**
The most likely negative outcome is a soggy or greasy crust that does not brown well. All-purpose flour contains starches that gelatinize and form a structured, crispy crust during frying, and proteins that contribute to deep browning via the Maillard reaction. Almond flour is high in fat and lacks starch. It will absorb oil rather than creating a dry, crispy barrier, and it will burn before it develops the same kind of browned, complex flavor.

**Answer 3**
Refined coconut oil is the better substitute. The function of "mounting" with cold butter is to create a stable emulsion that gives the soup a velvety texture, added richness (from fat), and a slight sheen.
*   **Why coconut oil is better:** It is solid at room temperature, similar to butter. When whisked into the hot soup, it will emulsify more effectively than a liquid oil, providing the desired body and creamy texture.
*   **Key difference:** While it will replicate the texture, refined coconut oil is a pure fat. Butter is an emulsion of fat, water, and milk solids. The milk solids in butter contribute subtle flavor and browning notes that the coconut oil will not provide. The final soup will have the richness but will lack that specific "buttery" flavor.

**Answer 4**
You will likely need to decrease the total amount of oil used relative to the aquafaba. An emulsion is a stable mixture of fat and water, facilitated by an emulsifier. In traditional mayonnaise, the egg yolk is very effective, containing lecithin that binds strongly to both oil and water. Aquafaba can create an emulsion, but its proteins are less efficient emulsifiers than lecithin. It is also more watery than an egg yolk. To create a stable emulsion with a weaker, more dilute emulsifier, you must reduce the proportion of fat (oil) to water (aquafaba), otherwise the emulsion will break, resulting in a greasy, separated mixture.

**Answer 5**
Here is one set of effective substitutions:
1.  **For All-Purpose Flour, substitute Bread Flour:** Bread flour has a higher protein content than all-purpose flour. More protein develops more gluten, which results in a chewier, more structured cookie rather than a crumbly or cakey one.
2.  **For Granulated White Sugar, substitute half of it with Brown Sugar:** White sugar contributes sweetness and crispness. Brown sugar contains molasses, which is acidic and hygroscopic (attracts water). The acidity reacts with baking soda to add lift, while its hygroscopic nature retains moisture, resulting in a much softer, chewier cookie. The molasses also adds a deeper, caramel-like flavor complexity.
3.  **For Unsalted Butter, substitute Browned Butter (Beurre Noisette):** Browning the butter before incorporating it cooks the milk solids, creating a deep, nutty, and complex flavor that standard melted or creamed butter lacks. This directly addresses the goal of a more complex flavor profile, transforming the butter from a simple fat into a major flavor component.

**Answer 6**
The two functional roles of egg yolks that cornstarch cannot replicate are **emulsification** and **enrichment (adding fat and flavor)**.
1.  **Missing Function: Emulsification.** Egg yolks contain lecithin, a powerful emulsifier that binds the fats (from butter) and liquids (lemon juice) into a smooth, stable, and creamy curd. Cornstarch is a starch-based thickener and has no emulsifying properties. The resulting curd might be thick, but it could have a greasy or separated texture.
    *   **Compensation:** To compensate, one could add a plant-based emulsifier like a small amount of soy lecithin powder, or rely on vigorous whisking and a higher-speed blending process to create a temporary mechanical emulsion.
2.  **Missing Function: Enrichment.** Egg yolks are rich in fat and protein, which gives traditional curd its distinctive richness, custardy flavor, and deep yellow color. Cornstarch provides no fat, flavor, or color. An egg-free curd thickened only with cornstarch would be pale and have a somewhat bland, starchy taste and a more translucent, gel-like texture.
    *   **Compensation:** To compensate for the lack of fat and flavor, you would need to add a rich, dairy-free fat source like coconut cream or a high-quality vegan butter at the end of cooking. For color, a tiny pinch of turmeric or a natural vegan food coloring could be used to mimic the yellow hue.
