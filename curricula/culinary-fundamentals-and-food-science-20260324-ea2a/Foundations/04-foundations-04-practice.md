## Exercises

**Exercise 1**
A simple vinaigrette is made by whisking olive oil, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt. Using the specific vocabulary from the lesson, describe the primary taste and texture characteristics you would expect from this dressing.

**Exercise 2**
Imagine you have two apples: a Granny Smith and a Red Delicious. Using the sensory vocabulary for taste and texture, describe one key difference you would expect to find between them when you take a bite.

**Exercise 3**
A cook is preparing a large batch of chicken broth. After tasting it, they comment that the broth has a good savory flavor from the chicken and vegetables, but it feels "hollow" or "thin" on the palate. What specific taste is likely underdeveloped, and what single, common ingredient could they add to fix this?

**Exercise 5**
A recipe for a pan-seared salmon fillet instructs the cook to finish the dish with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and a pat of butter that melts over the top. Analyze this finishing step. Identify the specific contributions (in terms of taste and texture) that the lemon juice and the butter each make to the final dish.

**Exercise 6**
You are tasked with improving a simple dessert of sliced strawberries. The strawberries are perfectly ripe, so they are already sweet and tender. Your goal is to make the dessert more complex and memorable by adding two more components. From the following list, choose two ingredients and justify your choice by explaining how they would combine with the strawberries to create a balanced sensory experience, referencing at least three distinct terms (taste, aroma, texture).

Available ingredients: Balsamic glaze, Crushed pistachios, Sea salt, Heavy cream

## Answer Key

**Answer 1**
The primary taste characteristics would be **sour** from the lemon juice and **salty** from the salt. The texture would be **creamy** or smooth, assuming the oil and lemon juice are properly emulsified by whisking.

*Reasoning:* The question asks for the primary taste and texture. Lemon juice is a classic acidic ingredient, providing sourness. Salt provides saltiness. When oil and an acid like lemon juice are whisked together, they form a temporary emulsion, which gives the dressing a smooth, creamy mouthfeel rather than the thinness of its separate parts.

**Answer 2**
The key difference would be in taste and texture. The Granny Smith apple would be predominantly **sour** with a very **crisp** texture. The Red Delicious would be primarily **sweet** with a softer, less crisp, or mealy texture.

*Reasoning:* This exercise requires applying the vocabulary to compare two common items. Granny Smith apples are known for their tartness (sourness) and firm, crisp flesh. Red Delicious apples are known for their mild sweetness and have a characteristically less firm texture that can sometimes be described as mealy or soft, which is the opposite of crisp.

**Answer 3**
The underdeveloped taste is **salty**. The cook should add salt.

*Reasoning:* A "hollow" or "thin" flavor in savory dishes like broth, even when umami is present, is a classic indicator of insufficient salt. Salt enhances and deepens existing flavors, making them taste more complete and robust. While other ingredients could add flavor, salt is the most direct and common solution for this specific problem.

**Answer 4**
A good topping would be toasted breadcrumbs or crispy fried onions.

*Reasoning:* The problem identifies the dish as texturally monotonous, despite having a good creamy texture. The solution is to add a contrasting texture. Toasted breadcrumbs would provide a **crisp** and slightly crunchy texture that would stand out against the smooth, **creamy** sauce and **tender** pasta, making each bite more interesting.

**Answer 5**
- **Lemon Juice:** This adds a **sour** taste. This acidity cuts through the richness of the fatty salmon, brightens the overall flavor profile, and prevents the dish from tasting too heavy.
- **Butter:** This adds a **creamy** texture and richness to the mouthfeel. As it melts, it forms a simple sauce that coats the salmon, enhancing its tenderness.

*Reasoning:* This question requires analyzing the function of each finishing ingredient. Lemon juice is an acid, primarily contributing a sour taste that balances fat and richness. Butter is a fat, which primarily contributes to a creamy texture and luxurious mouthfeel.

**Answer 6**
A strong choice would be the **balsamic glaze** and the **crushed pistachios**.

*Reasoning:* This combination creates a complex and balanced sensory experience.
1.  **Taste:** The balsamic glaze adds a new dimension of **sour** taste, which will contrast with and highlight the **sweet** taste of the strawberries. This prevents the dessert from being one-dimensionally sweet.
2.  **Texture:** The strawberries are **tender**. The crushed pistachios introduce a much-needed **crisp**, crunchy texture, making the dessert more engaging to eat.
3.  **Aroma/Flavor:** Pistachios add a nutty **aroma** and flavor, while the aged balsamic adds a deep, complex flavor that complements the fruit. The combination of sweet, sour, tender, and crisp elements results in a well-rounded and sophisticated dessert.
